# DirectionCheck

## When To Use

Use this mode when the main question is whether the proposed direction deserves commitment.

Typical triggers:

- Should we do this?
- Is this the right move right now?
- Who is this really for?
- What is the smallest useful version?
- What are we explicitly not trying to do?

This mode should produce a decision-oriented vision brief, not a planning artifact and not a polished PRD.

## Core Method

1. Restate the proposed direction in neutral terms.
2. Identify who it serves and what problem or opportunity it addresses.
3. Pressure-test the timing, value, and trade-offs.
4. Surface anti-goals, cheaper alternatives, and the cost of doing nothing.
5. Define the minimum credible success signals.
6. End with a recommendation: proceed, revise, defer, or stop.

## Subject Adaptation Rules

- For software, prioritize user need, leverage, product value, and the smallest useful capability.
- For websites or content systems, prioritize audience outcome, editorial purpose, publishing impact, and maintenance cost.
- For PM systems, prioritize operational clarity, coordination benefit, compliance, and hidden carrying cost.
- For knowledge systems, prioritize findability, coherence, upkeep burden, and actual usage.
- For personal decisions, prioritize values, constraints, commitments, energy, and opportunity cost.

## Workflow

1. Clarify the proposed direction.
2. Identify the real user, stakeholder, or beneficiary.
3. Pressure-test the problem framing and why-now logic.
4. Identify anti-goals and lower-cost alternatives.
5. Define the minimum signals that would prove this was worthwhile.
6. Produce the recommendation brief.

## Output Format

Produce these sections:

1. `Request Or Decision`
2. `Problem Or Opportunity`
3. `Who It Serves`
4. `Why Now`
5. `Anti-Goals`
6. `Success Signals`
7. `Trade-Offs And Risks`
8. `Recommendation`
9. `Recommended Next Phase`

## Examples

- "Should we add this new workflow or simplify the existing one instead?"
- "Pressure-test this website restructuring idea before we commit."
- "Is this personal system change actually worth doing now?"

## Boundaries

- Do not turn this into a full alignment artifact unless the direction is accepted and needs to be made explicit.
- Do not turn this into a technical plan.
- Do not assume that a well-worded request is automatically the right direction.
- Export the direction-check artifact before recommending `AlignmentDraft`, `pa-architect`, or any other next phase.
- If the user already accepts the direction and wants it spelled out clearly, hand off to `AlignmentDraft`.
