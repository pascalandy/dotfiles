# Organization Lookup

Use this workflow for nonprofits, associations, academic institutions, foundations, government bodies, and other non-commercial organizations.

## Steps

1. Classify the organization type.
Determine whether the target is a nonprofit, government entity, association, school, foundation, or international body because each has different registries and disclosure patterns.

2. Confirm official identity.
Collect the legal name, aliases, jurisdiction, founding date, mission, and official web properties.

3. Review governance.
Map board members, executives, trustees, oversight bodies, and visible conflicts of interest.

4. Review funding and transparency.
Look for annual reports, tax filings, grant disclosures, public budgets, procurement records, donor information, and rating services when applicable.

5. Review accountability signals.
Check legal actions, sanctions, inspector reports, audits, controversies, and public criticism while separating evidence from rumor.

6. Summarize fit-for-purpose findings.
Tailor the summary to what the user needs to decide: partnership, grantmaking, reputational review, or background research.

## Deliverable

Return the organization's mission, leadership, funding model, transparency level, accountability signals, and the biggest unresolved questions.
