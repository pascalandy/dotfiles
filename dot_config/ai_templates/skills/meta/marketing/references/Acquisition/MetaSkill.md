## Email Sequences

### Core Principles
1. One email, one job (single CTA per email)
2. Value before ask (build trust through content)
3. Relevance over volume (fewer, better emails)
4. Clear path forward (every email moves them somewhere)

### Sequence Types

| Type | Length | Timing | Goal |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| Welcome | 5-7 emails / 12-14 days | Immediate → day 2 → day 4... | Activate, build trust, convert |
| Lead Nurture | 6-8 emails / 2-3 weeks | Day 0 → day 3 → day 5... | Build trust, demonstrate expertise |
| Re-engagement | 3-4 emails / 2 weeks | Triggered 30-60 days inactive | Win back or clean list |
| Onboarding | 5-7 emails / 14 days | Coordinate with in-app | Activate, drive to aha moment |

### Subject Lines
Clear > Clever. Specific > Vague. 40-60 characters. Patterns: question, how-to, number, direct, story tease.

### Email Copy Structure
Hook (grab attention) → Context (why this matters) → Value (useful content) → CTA (what to do) → Sign-off (human, warm).

## Cold Email

### Writing Principles
- Write like a peer, not a vendor
- Every sentence must earn its place
- Personalization must connect to the problem
- Lead with their world, not yours
- One ask, low friction

### Voice
Target: a smart colleague who noticed something relevant. Conversational but not sloppy. Calibrate to audience (C-suite: ultra-brief; mid-level: more detail; technical: precise, no fluff).

### Structure Options
- Observation → Problem → Proof → Ask
- Question → Value → Ask
- Trigger → Insight → Ask
- Story → Bridge → Ask

### Subject Lines
2-4 words, lowercase, no punctuation tricks. Should look like it came from a colleague.

### Follow-Up
3-5 total emails, increasing gaps. Each adds something new (different angle, fresh proof, useful resource). Never "just checking in."

### Quality Check
Does it sound human? Would YOU reply? Does every sentence serve the reader? Is there one clear, low-friction ask?

## Lead Magnets

### Principles
1. Solve a specific problem (not a broad topic)
2. Match the buyer stage (awareness → education; decision → implementation)
3. High perceived value, low time investment (under 30 minutes, ideally under 10)
4. Natural path to product
5. Easy to consume

### Types by Effort

| Type | Effort | Time to Create |
|------|--------|----------------|
| Checklist / Cheat sheet | Low | 1-4 hours |
| Template | Low-Med | 2-8 hours |
| Swipe file | Medium | 4-8 hours |
| Ebook / Guide | High | 1-3 weeks |
| Mini-course | Medium-High | 1-4 weeks |
| Quiz / Assessment | Medium | 1-2 weeks |

### Gating Strategy
- Email only: highest conversion
- Email + name: enables personalization
- Email + company/role: better qualification, more friction
- Each extra field reduces conversion 5-10%

### Landing Page Structure
Headline (benefit) → Preview/mockup → What's inside (3-5 bullets) → Social proof → Form → FAQ

See [references/lead-magnet-format-guide.md](references/lead-magnet-format-guide.md), [references/lead-magnet-benchmarks.md](references/lead-magnet-benchmarks.md).

## Paid Ads

### Platform Selection

| Platform | Best For | Use When |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Google Ads | High-intent search | People actively search for your solution |
| Meta | Demand generation, visual | Creating demand, strong creative assets |
| LinkedIn | B2B, decision-makers | Job title/company targeting matters |
| TikTok | Younger demographics | Audience skews 18-34, video capacity |

### Campaign Structure
Account → Campaign (Objective + Audience) → Ad Set (Targeting variation) → Ads (Creative variations)

### Ad Copy Frameworks
- **PAS:** Problem → Agitate → Solve → CTA
- **BAB:** Before → After → Bridge (your product)
- **Social Proof Lead:** Impressive stat → What you do → CTA

### Optimization
If CPA too high: check landing page → tighten targeting → test new creative → improve relevance → adjust bids.

### Retargeting
Top funnel (blog readers): educational, social proof. Middle (pricing visitors): case studies, demos. Bottom (cart/trial): urgency, objection handling.

## Ad Creative

### Generate from Scratch
1. Define 3-5 distinct angles (pain point, outcome, social proof, curiosity, comparison, urgency, identity, contrarian)
2. Generate multiple variations per angle (vary word choice, specificity, tone, structure)
3. Validate against platform character limits
4. Organize for upload

### Iterate from Performance Data
1. Analyze winners (themes, structures, word patterns)
2. Analyze losers (what falls flat)
3. Double down on winning themes, test 1-2 new angles, avoid underperformer patterns

### Platform Specs
Google RSA: 30 char headlines (up to 15), 90 char descriptions (up to 4). Meta: 125 char primary text visible, 40 char headline. LinkedIn: 150 char intro, 70 char headline.

See [references/platform-specs.md](references/platform-specs.md), [references/generative-tools.md](references/generative-tools.md).

## Launch Strategy

### ORB Framework
- **Owned:** Email list, blog, social, product, community
- **Rented:** Social platforms, Product Hunt, review sites, directories
- **Borrowed:** Press, partnerships, influencers, guest posts, affiliates

### Five Phases
1. **Build anticipation** (4-8 weeks out): Tease, waitlist, early access
2. **Prepare assets** (2-4 weeks): Landing page, emails, social content, press kit
3. **Soft launch** (1 week): Beta users, collect feedback, fix issues
4. **Launch day**: Coordinated push across all channels
5. **Post-launch momentum** (2-4 weeks): Follow-up content, case studies, optimizations

### Product Hunt
Submit Tuesday-Thursday, prepare screenshots and description, rally early supporters, engage with comments all day.
