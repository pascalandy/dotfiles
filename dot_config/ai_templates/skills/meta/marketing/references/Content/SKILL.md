## Copywriting

### Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness** -- if you must choose, choose clear
2. **Benefits over features** -- what it means for the customer, not what it does
3. **Specificity over vagueness** -- "Cut reporting from 4 hours to 15 minutes" not "Save time"
4. **Customer language over company language** -- mirror voice-of-customer
5. **One idea per section** -- build a logical flow down the page

### Writing Style
- Simple over complex ("Use" not "utilize")
- Active over passive ("We generate reports" not "Reports are generated")
- Confident over qualified (remove "almost," "very," "really")
- Honest over sensational (no fabricated statistics)

### Page Structure

**Above the Fold:**
- Headline (core value proposition, specific > generic)
- Subheadline (expands, adds specificity, 1-2 sentences)
- Primary CTA (action-oriented, communicate what they get)

**Core Sections:** Social Proof → Problem/Pain → Solution/Benefits → How It Works → Objection Handling → Final CTA

### Headline Formulas
- "{Achieve outcome} without {pain point}"
- "The {category} for {audience}"
- "Never {unpleasant event} again"

### CTA Copy
Weak: Submit, Sign Up, Learn More. Strong: Start Free Trial, Get [Specific Thing], See [Product] in Action.

Formula: [Action Verb] + [What They Get] + [Qualifier]

### Output
Page copy organized by section, annotations explaining choices, 2-3 alternatives for headlines and CTAs, meta content if relevant.

## Copy Editing (Seven Sweeps Framework)

Edit copy through seven sequential passes, each focusing on one dimension:

### Sweep 1: Clarity
Can the reader understand what you're saying? Check for confusing structures, unclear references, jargon, ambiguity.

### Sweep 2: Voice and Tone
Is the copy consistent in how it sounds? Check for shifts between formal/casual, inconsistent personality, jarring mood changes.

### Sweep 3: So What
Does every claim answer "why should I care?" Test every statement: if it doesn't bridge to a deeper benefit, it needs work.

### Sweep 4: Prove It
Is every claim supported with evidence? Flag unsubstantiated assertions. Add testimonials, statistics, third-party validation.

### Sweep 5: Specificity
Is the copy concrete enough? Replace vague language with numbers, timeframes, examples. "Save time" → "Save 4 hours every week."

### Sweep 6: Heightened Emotion
Does the copy make the reader feel something? Paint the "before" state vividly. Use sensory language and micro-stories.

### Sweep 7: Zero Risk
Have we removed every barrier to action? Check for friction near CTAs, unanswered objections, missing trust signals, unclear next steps.

### Word-Level Quick Fixes
Cut: very, really, just, actually, basically, in order to. Replace: utilize→use, leverage→use, facilitate→help, innovative→new, robust→strong, seamless→smooth.

See [references/plain-english-alternatives.md](references/plain-english-alternatives.md).

## Content Strategy

### Searchable vs Shareable

Every piece must be searchable, shareable, or both. Prioritize search -- it captures existing demand.

**Searchable:** Target specific keywords, match search intent, structure with headings matching query patterns, optimize for AI/LLM discovery.

**Shareable:** Lead with novel insight or original data, challenge conventional wisdom, tell stories, create content people share to look smart.

### Content Types

**Searchable:** Use-case content ("[persona] + [use-case]"), Hub and spoke (comprehensive overview + subtopics), Template libraries

**Shareable:** Thought leadership, data-driven content, expert roundups, case studies, behind-the-scenes transparency

### Content Pillars
3-5 core topics your brand will own. Identify via: product-led (what problems you solve), audience-led (what ICP needs to learn), search-led (volume in your space), competitor-led (gaps they leave).

### Keyword Research by Buyer Stage
- **Awareness:** "what is," "how to," "guide to"
- **Consideration:** "best," "top," "vs," "alternatives"
- **Decision:** "pricing," "reviews," "demo," "trial"
- **Implementation:** "templates," "examples," "tutorial," "setup"

### Prioritization
Score each idea: Customer Impact (40%) + Content-Market Fit (30%) + Search Potential (20%) + Resources (10%).

## Social Content

### Platform Reference

| Platform | Best For | Frequency | Key Format |
|----------|----------|-----------|------------|
| LinkedIn | B2B, thought leadership | 3-5x/week | Carousels, stories |
| Twitter/X | Tech, real-time, community | 3-10x/day | Threads, hot takes |
| Instagram | Visual brands, lifestyle | 1-2 posts + Stories daily | Reels, carousels |
| TikTok | Brand awareness, younger | 1-4x/day | Short-form video |

### Content Pillars
Build around 3-5 pillars. Example: Industry insights (30%), Behind-the-scenes (25%), Educational (25%), Personal (15%), Promotional (5%).

### Hook Formulas
- **Curiosity:** "I was wrong about [common belief]."
- **Story:** "Last week, [unexpected thing] happened."
- **Value:** "How to [outcome] without [pain]:"
- **Contrarian:** "Unpopular opinion: [bold statement]"

### Repurposing System
Blog Post → LinkedIn key insight + carousel → Twitter thread → Instagram carousel → Reel summary.

### Engagement Routine (30 min/day)
Respond to comments (5 min) → Comment on 5-10 target accounts (15 min) → Share/repost with insight (5 min) → 2-3 DMs (5 min).

### Reverse Engineering
Find 10-20 high-engagement creators → Collect 500+ posts → Analyze patterns → Codify playbook → Layer your voice.
