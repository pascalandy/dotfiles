# The Four Laws — Portable Predictors

> Four named laws that transfer across scales (interpersonal, organizational, national, civilizational). Each gives a formal predictive structure for a recurring pattern in human conflict.

## Core concept

The fundamentals tell you how to see a game. The laws tell you how the game usually *resolves* when stress is applied. All four are diagnostics first and predictors second — they sharpen reasoning about who wins, when, and how.

## The four laws

### 1. Law of Asymmetry

> *Empires' advantages decay into their opposites. The insurgent's disadvantages decay into advantages.*

- **Empire advantages**: mass (manpower, materiel), organization (bureaucracy, logistics), depth (reserves, time horizons).
- **Decay path**: mass → complacency; organization → rent-seeking; depth → hubris.
- **Insurgent path**: scarcity → energy; marginalization → openness to innovation; shared oppression → cohesion.
- **Prediction rule**: in a long enough conflict, the EOC triad shifts toward the insurgent. Empire strategies that *look* decisive (decapitation, air supremacy, arming proxies) usually *accelerate* insurgent EOC gains.

### 2. Law of Escalation

> *Escalation control beats escalation dominance. The ladder must be climbed sequentially; skipping rungs almost always backfires.*

- **Escalation dominance** = "I can hit you harder at every level." Brittle. Invites maximal response.
- **Escalation control** = "I choose the tempo and level of conflict, and I can de-escalate." Durable.
- **Four dimensions of war** — narrative, political, economic, military — any of which can be escalated independently.
- **Cost pyramid** — infantry base, air top. Winning requires the full stack; skipping the base is a common imperial mistake.
- **Prediction rule**: the side with more calibration options (more rungs, more dimensions) wins protracted conflicts. Mission creep is the signature of losing escalation control.

### 3. Law of Eschatological Convergence

> *Where multiple extremist religious or ideological scripts converge on the same event, expect that event.*

- **Formula**: *Winner = Mass × Energy × Coordination*, where coordination is ~4× weighted over mass.
- **Mechanism**: extreme vectors are more *coordinated* than mainstream moderates; coordinated minorities pull the mainstream toward their vector.
- **Diagnostic**: map each relevant eschatology (Zoroastrian, Jewish messianic, Christian-Zionist premillennial, Freemason, Islamic, Catholic, Orthodox — see lecture 12) and look for the intersection of predicted end-state events. That intersection is the attractor.
- **Prediction rule**: long-horizon geopolitical events cluster at eschatological convergence points, not at the "center of gravity" of public opinion.

### 4. Law of Proximity

> *The game closest to you dominates your behavior. Internal games (factions at home) beat external games (foreign threats) for almost all actors.*

- Leaders are killed/removed by their *own* faction far more often than by foreign enemies — external threats are used *as tools* in internal fights.
- A diagnostic for any decision: whose approval does the agent need *today* to survive? That faction's game is the one driving the decision.
- **Bubble diagnostic**: each faction depends on particular economic/political bubbles for bailout. When a bubble threatens to pop, the faction acts to extend it, even at external cost.
- **Prediction rule**: in any multi-level game, predict behavior by identifying the *closest* decisive audience, not the largest or most distant one.

## Workflow: applying the laws

1. **Classify the conflict.** Is it asymmetric (empire vs. insurgent)? Protracted? Religiously/ideologically loaded? Multi-level (factions within factions)?
2. **Select applicable laws.** Usually more than one applies.
3. **Apply each law mechanically.**
   - Asymmetry → read the EOC triad on both sides; trace decay paths.
   - Escalation → enumerate rungs across all four dimensions; identify who has more calibration.
   - Eschatological convergence → map scripts; find the intersection.
   - Proximity → for each decisive agent, identify the closest faction they cannot survive without.
4. **Compose.** Different laws often point different directions. When they converge on the same prediction, confidence is high.
5. **Flag the counter-case.** For each prediction, state the superstructure change that would invalidate it.

## Output format

- **Conflict classification.**
- **Applicable laws:** bulleted, with brief justification.
- **Per-law analysis:** each law → predicted outcome + key uncertainty.
- **Composed prediction:** single most-likely trajectory.
- **Counter-case:** what would flip it.
