## Geopolitics, Civil Conflicts, and the Law of Proximity

### Overview

This is a university lecture analyzing an escalating war between Israel and Iran. The lecturer uses the conflict to introduce the **law of proximity**—the idea that internal conflicts within nations matter more than external ones—and applies it to America, Israel, and Iran to explain why leaders are being killed, why the war has no off-ramp, and where the world is heading.

---

### War Update: Major Escalation

#### Main Idea

The war between Israel and Iran has escalated significantly. Israel bombed Iranian oil fields. Iran retaliated by attacking the energy infrastructure of the GCC (Gulf Cooperation Council) states and striking Tel Aviv with cluster warheads that air defense systems cannot stop.

#### Supporting Material: Qatar and Tel Aviv Strikes

- Qatar provides 20% of the world's LNG and was targeted
- Tel Aviv is being hit with cluster warheads; footage is being censored
- Iran's entire strategy is framed as destroying the global economy by targeting energy infrastructure

**Connection**: The strikes on GCC energy infrastructure illustrate Iran's maximalist strategy—not just hitting Israel, but threatening the global economy's foundation: cheap energy.

#### Supporting Material: The Rabbi in Jerusalem

One of the more influential rabbis in Jerusalem is shown on video saying this war of destruction will bring the coming of the Messiah, predicted for "Thursday" (the day of the lecture). Rather than being saddened by the destruction of Tel Aviv, these religious figures are animated and excited.

**Connection**: This is the lecturer's first signal that the war cannot be understood through a purely geopolitical lens. He argues it must be viewed through an **eschatological** perspective—one concerned with end times and divine purpose.

> "You cannot look at this war from a geopolitical lens. You have to look at it from an eschatological perspective."

### The Strategic Map: Maximalist Objectives on Both Sides

#### Main Idea

Both sides are pursuing maximalist objectives with no interest in compromise. The US and Israel want to destroy Iran's capacity to function as a nation-state. Iran wants to destroy the global economy to restructure the world order.

#### US/Israel Strategy

- **Decapitation strikes**: killing Iranian leadership
- **Economic targeting**: destroying Iran's economy
- **Future escalation**: targeting civilian critical infrastructure including water
- **Balkanization**: provoking ethnic conflict to divide Iran into ethnic enclaves

#### Iran Strategy

- Attacking GCC energy infrastructure to cut the world off from cheap energy
- Activating proxies (Houthis) to close maritime choke points
- Goal: isolate the GCC and force a global energy crisis

#### Supporting Material: Kharg Island and Mission Creep

**Kharg Island** stores about 9% of Iran's oil exports. The lecturer predicts the Americans will eventually launch a ground invasion there. The problem: it's easy to take but hard to defend because of the coastline. Defending it requires taking the coastline, which then requires pushing into the Zagros Mountains, which requires expanding further outward.

**Connection**: This illustrates **mission creep**—where initial military objectives expand to justify continued war. The lecturer flags Kharg Island as a flash point to watch in the news.

#### Supporting Material: Pakistan's Entry

Saudi Arabia is being attacked by Iran. If Saudi Arabia declares war, it triggers a mutual defense pact with Pakistan. Pakistan opens the eastern front against Iran and—critically—possesses nuclear weapons.

> "Once Pakistan is in the war, nuclear warheads are now at play."

The lecturer states he strongly believes tactical nuclear weapons will not be used but acknowledges they become a factor once Pakistan enters.

#### Supporting Material: Japan and South Korea

If maritime choke points are closed and GCC energy is cut off, nations heavily dependent on that energy—Japan and South Korea—may be forced into the war.

### The Netanyahu Question

#### Main Idea

There is online speculation that Benjamin Netanyahu is dead. He has not been seen in public for many days, has been absent from cabinet meetings he normally chairs, and AI-generated videos of him giving speeches have been identified as fake.

#### Supporting Material: The Café Photo

A photo was released showing Netanyahu visiting a café in Jerusalem, supposedly to prove he's alive. The lecturer notes the café is in a mountain area, roughly 50 minutes by car, and asks why Netanyahu would go there instead of simply holding a cabinet meeting or calling Donald Trump.

**Connection**: The lecturer personally does not believe Netanyahu is dead but suggests he may be hiding or injured. The Iranians have successfully killed senior Israeli officials, including the head of Mossad, and targeted Netanyahu's residence in Tel Aviv. This raises questions about Iranian intelligence capabilities.

### The Assassination of Ali Lerajani

#### Main Idea

Ali Lerajani, the de facto head of Iran's war effort, was assassinated and confirmed dead. Analysts are extremely worried because he was pragmatic, experienced, and influential enough to negotiate a ceasefire by uniting the different factions of the Iranian government.

> "With him dead, it is now almost impossible to foresee a ceasefire."

#### Supporting Material: The Gang War Analogy

The lecturer uses a street gang analogy. Two gangs at war avoid killing each other's leaders for two reasons:

1. **Treaty authority**: Leaders can implement peace agreements. Without them, no one has authority to negotiate.
2. **Replacement dynamics**: When a leader dies during a war, the most violent and extreme person rises to replace them, causing massive escalation with no way out.

**Connection**: By killing Lerajani, the Israelis and Americans have ensured there is no off-ramp. The war will be fought to the bitter end—which, the lecturer argues, is the actual objective. Both sides are pursuing maximalist aims.

> "By targeting Lerigeni, the Israelis and the Americans have ensured that there will be no off ramp now."

### Three Big Questions

#### Main Idea

The lecturer frames three central questions for the rest of the lecture:

1. **How** are these leaders dying? (Intelligence methods)
2. **Why** are they being killed? (Strategic purpose)
3. **What does this mean** for the war and the world?

#### Intelligence Methods: HUMINT vs SIGINT

Two ways to locate leaders:

- **HUMINT** (Human Intelligence): spies embedded in local networks
- **SIGINT** (Signal Intelligence): electronic eavesdropping, phone tracking, IP tracking

The Americans claim they use electronic surveillance. The lecturer finds this unconvincing and believes the most reliable source is HUMINT—having spies embedded locally who can report precise locations.

This raises a deeper question the lecturer returns to later: how are both sides getting such good human intelligence on each other?

### The Law of Proximity

#### Main Idea

The **law of proximity** is introduced as a new conceptual framework. The idea: whenever you play a game, you're actually playing many games simultaneously. The game that impacts your decision-making the most is the one closest to you—the most proximate one.

#### Supporting Material: The Everyday Life Analogy

The lecturer maps out the multiple games a person plays daily:

1. **Family game**: competing with siblings for parental attention (strategies include doing well in school or being difficult)
2. **Parents' game**: competing with other parents for social prestige
3. **School game**: competing with classmates for popularity and grades
4. **Work game**: competing for the boss's favor and colleague approval
5. **City game**: wanting your city (e.g., Beijing) to be better than rivals (Shanghai, Shenzhen)
6. **National game**: wanting your country to be stronger than others

**Connection**: The game closest to you dominates your behavior, even if you don't recognize it. This applies to nations too—the conflict *within* nations determines how they behave against each other.

> "We think that the game is between nations, but really the game is within nations."

### Civil Conflict in America

#### Main Idea

America has a civil conflict operating on two levels: Democrats vs. Republicans on the surface, and a deeper conflict between the elite and the counter-elite.

#### Democrats' Strategy

The Democrats support the war because they believe it will be extremely unpopular, allowing them to dominate the midterms and win the 2028 presidential election. They believe it will destroy Trump and the Republican party permanently.

#### Republicans' Strategy

The Republicans accept the war may be unpopular and may cause America to lose, but they have tools to retain power:

- **Emergency Powers Act**: allows the president to suspend the constitution and delay elections during wartime
- **SAFE America Act**: requires voter ID, which historically has been used to discriminate against minorities and suppress their vote
- **National draft**: if the war goes badly, they could call a draft and cancel elections

#### The Deeper Divide: Elite vs. Counter-Elite

The lecturer invokes **Peter Turchin's** concept of **elite overproduction**: too many elites competing for a zero-sum amount of power, which historically leads to civil war.

| | Elite (Democrats) | Counter-Elite (Republicans) |
|---|---|---|
| Vision | Maintain global order (Empire) | Retreat to Western Hemisphere (MAGA / America First) |
| Financial backers | Wall Street (Finance) | Silicon Valley (AI/Tech) |
| Disposition | Status quo | Revolution |

#### Supporting Material: Two Parasitic Bubbles

Both finance and AI are described as bubbles—parasites expecting government bailouts:

**Private Credit Bubble (~$2 trillion)**:
- Companies privately issuing loans to other companies instead of going through banks
- Reckless lending because of **moral hazard**—after the 2008 bailout, they suffered no consequences for stupidity
- When companies can't repay, they lend them more money to stay afloat
- Mentality: "We control Washington, so the government will bail us out"

**AI Bubble**:
- Companies like Microsoft, OpenAI, Oracle, Nvidia lending money to each other in circular arrangements

> "I give you a billion dollars, then you give me a billion dollars, and now I have two billion dollars. It's a circle jerk."

- Getting heavy investment from GCC—which is now threatened by the war
- Same mentality: "We control Trump, we are the stock market, Washington will bail us out"

**Connection**: The government doesn't have infinite money. It can only bail out one side. If Democrats win in 2028, the private credit people get bailed out. If Trump stays in power, the AI people get bailed out. This is the real civil war in America right now.

### Civil Conflict in Israel

#### Main Idea

Israel is deeply divided, and always has been. The major divide is between Tel Aviv and Jerusalem, which represent two fundamentally different visions for the country.

| | Tel Aviv | Jerusalem |
|---|---|---|
| Governance | Democratic | Theocratic |
| Culture | Open, cosmopolitan, secular | Conservative, traditional, religious |
| Social | Progressive, large LGBTQ community | Socially conservative |
| Orientation | Western, outward-looking | Inward-looking, God-focused |

#### Supporting Material: Netanyahu's Corruption and October 7th

Before October 7th, approximately a million people in Tel Aviv were protesting Netanyahu's corruption and his attempts to change the judiciary to avoid prison. He was on the verge of falling from power. October 7th gave him emergency powers and permanent war, saving his political career.

#### Supporting Material: The Knesset's Fragmentation

The Israeli parliament has many political parties, none dominant. The largest (Likud) has about 30 members and must form coalitions. The lecturer emphasizes that the division is genuine—these political leaders truly hate each other.

#### Supporting Material: Historical Jewish Factionalism

The lecturer traces internal Jewish conflict back to the time of Jesus, citing three major factions in Jerusalem: the **Sadducees**, the **Pharisees**, and the **Essenes**. In 70 AD, when the Romans besieged Jerusalem, the factions refused to unite even facing annihilation. The Romans destroyed Jerusalem, burned the Second Temple, and massacred many Jews.

> "The Jews have always had this problem where they're extremely creative and intellectual, but that just leads to massive infighting."

#### Supporting Material: The Story of David and Bathsheba

Tel Aviv and Jerusalem both claim King David but interpret him differently:

**Tel Aviv's David**: The king at the height of Israel's glory—an open, cosmopolitan, creative, innovative empire. This is what they want Israel to be.

**Jerusalem's David**: A poet-prophet whose greatest achievement was redemption. The story:
- David falls in love with Bathsheba, who is married to Uriah the Hittite
- David has Uriah killed and takes Bathsheba
- God sends the prophet Nathan to confront David
- David and Bathsheba's child dies
- David falls into grief, prays daily for forgiveness
- God forgives him, and they become closer than ever

**Connection**: For the people of Jerusalem, this story is the template for Israel. The nation must go through sin, suffering, and destruction to achieve redemption and closeness to God. This is why they are not troubled by the destruction of Tel Aviv.

#### The Animal Soul vs. The Divine Soul

The religious camp frames the conflict using Kabbalistic concepts:

- **Animal soul** (Tel Aviv): seeks material comfort, sex, partying, luxury
- **Divine soul** (Jerusalem): seeks only to be with God

From this perspective, Tel Aviv represents the animal soul of Israel. Its destruction is purification. War and suffering are necessary because people must lose everything before recognizing God's glory—echoing the **Book of Job**, where Job's faith is tested and deepened through total loss.

The rabbi's prediction about the Messiah coming "Thursday" flows from this logic: so much destruction means the Jews will finally recognize that only God can save them, and when they truly want the Messiah in their hearts, that is when the Messiah will come.

### Civil Conflict in Iran

#### Main Idea

Iran has a dual identity—a religious theocracy with secular government structures underneath. The main conflict is between the **Islamic theocracy** (rural, religious) and **secular nationalists** (urban, educated, democratic).

#### Wartime Radicalization

The lecturer predicts that war will push both sides to more extreme positions:

- The Islamic theocracy will transition into **Shia eschatology/martyrdom**—believing this war is about the return of the **Twelfth Imam (Mahdi)**, their messiah, to fight the "Great Satan" (Israel and the US)
- The secular nationalists will become **Persian exceptionalists**—believing Persian civilization is the greatest in human history and worth dying for, with survival of the civilization prioritized over survival of the state

#### Key Question

Can Iran reconcile these two strands, or will it descend into civil war? Iran cannot afford internal conflict while being bombarded by Israel and America—but the law of proximity suggests people care more about internal conflicts than external ones.

---

### Answering the Three Questions

#### How Are Leaders Getting Killed?

The lecturer's answer: internal civil conflicts. The different factions within each nation are providing intelligence to their enemies to eliminate their domestic rivals.

> "These different factions are providing intelligence to their enemies in order to limit their internal enemies."

He acknowledges he has no direct evidence but argues this is the best explanation according to game theory. Both Iranians and Israelis are doing an unusually excellent job of killing each other's leaders—something that requires precise, local intelligence that foreign nations typically cannot obtain alone.

#### Why Are They Getting Killed?

Same answer: internal factions using external enemies as tools against domestic opponents. The proximate game (internal power struggle) matters more than the distant game (international war).

#### Where Is the World Going?

The lecturer sees a global shift from a **secular financial order** to a **nationalist theocratic order** over the next 5–10 years:

- Iran becomes more theocratic, extreme, and nationalistic
- Israel abandons democracy and embraces theocracy
- America also abandons democracy and moves toward theocracy

> "This is the general trend of the world."

### Q&A: The Animal Soul and the Divine Soul

#### Student Question

Can the animal soul / divine soul framework be applied to individuals? Are people always struggling between these two aspects?

#### Lecturer's Response

The framing is that of religious Israelis. People in Tel Aviv would frame the debate as progress vs. tradition, not animal vs. divine. But the lecturer acknowledges validity in the idea that modern society has been indulging the animal soul for 20 years—too materialistic, not spiritual enough.

He predicts the world is heading toward an economic depression, a splintering. People won't be able to afford luxury vacations or find avocados in the supermarket. But this forces introspection—asking what gives meaning and purpose. The answer, he suggests, is each other: community, family, reflection.

> "What gives me meaning and purpose in life? And the answer, of course, is each other."

He frames this as possibly the "intention of the universe"—that redemption comes through suffering, pain, and tragedy. The people of Jerusalem would call it the plan of God.

### Key Takeaways

- The **law of proximity** explains why internal conflicts shape nations' external behavior more than geopolitical strategy does
- **Killing leaders eliminates off-ramps**—it ensures wars escalate with no possibility of negotiated settlement
- Internal factions within nations likely provide intelligence to foreign enemies to weaken domestic rivals
- Both the finance sector and AI sector in America are parasitic bubbles competing for government bailouts
- The world is trending from secular liberal democracy toward nationalist theocracy across multiple nations simultaneously
- Watch for: Kharg Island as a military flash point, Pakistan's potential entry into the war, closure of maritime choke points by Houthis

### Key Terms Introduced

- **Law of Proximity**: the game closest to you is the one that most determines your behavior, even when larger games are at play
- **HUMINT**: human intelligence—spies embedded in local networks
- **SIGINT**: signal intelligence—electronic eavesdropping and surveillance
- **Mission Creep**: when military objectives expand incrementally, with each expansion justifying further involvement
- **Elite Overproduction**: Peter Turchin's theory that too many elites competing for limited power leads to civil conflict
- **Moral Hazard**: when lack of consequences for reckless behavior encourages more reckless behavior
- **Balkanization**: dividing a nation into smaller ethnic or sectarian enclaves
- **Eschatology**: theological framework concerned with end times, final judgment, and messianic return
- **Animal Soul / Divine Soul**: Kabbalistic concept distinguishing between material desires and the soul's yearning for God
- **Persian Exceptionalism**: belief that Persian civilization is the greatest in history and worth dying for
- **Decapitation Strikes**: targeted killings of a nation's leadership to destabilize command structure
