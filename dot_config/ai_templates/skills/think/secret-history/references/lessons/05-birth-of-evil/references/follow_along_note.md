## Secret Societies: Origins and Beliefs

### Overview

This is the second lecture in a series on secret societies. The previous class covered how they come into power. This lecture traces where secret societies come from and what they believe, using a sweeping history of western religion—from the mother goddess civilization through polytheism to monotheism—to show how suppressed ideas went underground. The second half examines the esoteric (apocryphal) reading of the Bible and uses John Milton's *Paradise Lost* to demonstrate how secret knowledge was embedded in literature.

---

### Three Stages of Religious Development in the Western World

#### Main Idea

The lecturer presents a simplified framework of three major stages of religious development in the western world, each tied to material and social conditions. These stages are essential context for understanding where secret societies originate.

The three stages:

- **Mother Goddess Civilization** — arose from agriculture
- **Polytheism** — arose from war ("many gods")
- **Monotheism** — arose from empire ("one god")

Each transition was driven by changing material conditions: agriculture → resource competition → imperial conquest.

---

### The Mother Goddess Civilization

#### Main Idea

The earliest religious system centered on the **womb** as a divine portal connecting the spirit world to the material world. This civilization emerged because agriculture created two fundamental problems: growing healthy crops and producing enough children to work the fields. Religion developed to address both.

Key features of this world:

- The **womb** is the center of religious life — considered a divine gateway from the spirit world into the material world
- **Women are in control** — they hold high status as direct representatives of the mother goddess
- **Everything is interconnected** — the womb, the stars, and the crops are aligned in a harmonized system
- **Astrology** emerges here — the study of stellar movement to understand its impact on earthly life (when to plant, when to have children)

#### Symbols of Worship

Two central symbols:

- **The Bird** — represents the mother goddess because birds have dominion over the sky, and if life comes from the sky, the goddess must be a bird
- **The Bull** — represents virility and male energy, since women cannot conceive alone

#### Social Structure

This world is radically different from ours:

- No property — everything belongs to everyone
- No hierarchy — everyone is equal
- Sex is a communal act, not a private one

**Fertility cults** were the main instrument of religious worship. Multiple men would have sex with one woman, the logic being that you want the best DNA, and since you don't know who has the best sperm, you should mate with everyone.

### The Transition to Polytheism

#### Main Idea

The shift from mother goddess civilization to polytheism was caused by **war**. As populations increased, competition for resources began. The most important resource was **women** — because women give birth. To incentivize men to fight, a new concept emerged: **property**. A wife now belonged to one man as a reward for fighting.

Three new concepts appear:

- **Property**
- **Hierarchy**
- **Marriage**

The role of women decreases. The role of men increases.

#### Supporting Material: The Origin of War

A student asks why men fight for women but women don't fight for men. The lecturer explains:

In the mother goddess world, women had all the status. As populations grew, young men with no families were discarded from societies due to lack of resources. These discarded men became like **wolf packs** — raiding other villages to capture women. This is presented as the origin of war.

> "These young men become like wolves... they become like a wolf pack who then go and pillage other villages. And that's how you have the origin of war."

#### The Pantheon of Gods

Different societies each had their own god. When societies went to war, it was as if the gods were fighting. Losing a war meant your god was inferior. To maintain peace, the loser admitted their god was weaker and submitted to the winner's god. This created **hierarchies of gods** — the **pantheon**.

**Connection**: The Greek pantheon illustrates this. Zeus sits at the top not because he was always supreme, but because the people who believed in Zeus won wars against those who believed in other gods. The defeated gods became Zeus' children — Apollo, Ares, Aphrodite, etc.

### The Transition to Monotheism

#### Main Idea

The shift from polytheism to monotheism was driven by **empire** — specifically, the Roman Empire and the introduction of Christianity. The lecturer claims Christianity is the first monotheistic religion (while acknowledging debate about Zoroastrianism and Judaism).

The key difference: in a war-based society, humans are still the most precious resource, so you avoid total destruction. But an empire like Rome doesn't need to worry about that — it can destroy entire societies and impose its god on everyone. This is how monotheism takes hold.

### How Worldviews Changed Over Time

#### Main Idea

The lecturer identifies two fundamental differences between the ancient worldview and the modern one. These differences are critical because secret societies exist to preserve the ancient understanding.

#### Difference 1: Mind Leads to Matter vs. Matter Leads to Mind

In the mother goddess civilization and throughout most of human history, people believed **mind leads to matter** — the mind creates the brain in order to understand the world.

In our modern world, we believe the opposite: **matter leads to mind** — the brain, through somatic connections, creates the mind.

The lecturer argues the ancient view actually makes more sense:

> "How does our small little brain — how is it able to do so much processing, which is the mind? How are we able to imagine? How are we able to dream?"

He claims that top neuroscientists, when confronted with this question, respond not with "we don't know" but with "don't ask this question" — it is forbidden in neuroscience.

**This is identified as the great secret that secret societies are trying to preserve: mind leads to matter.**

#### Difference 2: Metaphorical World vs. Literal World

Ancient people understood the world through **metaphors**. Modern people live in a **literal** world.

**Example**: If two people fight today, we say "I got angry, the emotion overwhelmed me, I hit him." The ancient metaphorical interpretation: anger is a god who seized me and propelled me into the fight, then unleashed the god of vengeance who seized the other person.

The lecturer argues the metaphorical understanding is more useful for interpreting the world and made ancient people far more creative than we are today.

> "Back then, they were much more creative than we are today."

**Connection**: The pyramids are cited as evidence — we can't figure out how they were built, and our default explanation is aliens, because "their mind is beyond our imagination."

### The Polytheistic Worldview in Detail

#### Main Idea

The polytheistic world maintained the mind-leads-to-matter framework but added layers of complexity. Four elements structure this worldview, from bottom to top:

1. **Humans are playthings of the gods** — gods like Zeus and Apollo are no different from mortals except they live forever (Zeus chases young women all day, just like a king would)
2. **Above the gods are the older gods** — Fortune and Fate control even the gods
3. **What matters is to be lucky** — being fortunate showed the gods favored you; being good was secondary
4. **The unwritten and immutable laws of the universe** — concepts like **justice** exist at the most fundamental level, like gravity gives structure to the physical universe. Even if gods favor you and you're lucky, acting unjustly will eventually bring punishment because you're breaking the structure of the universe

---

### The Monotheistic Worldview and Three New Dominant Concepts

#### Main Idea

The concept of **one true god** changes everything. It collapses the complex, diverse, imaginative polytheistic framework into a single system. Three concepts that had existed before now become dominant:

1. **Truth** — if there's one true God, there must be one truth to the universe. Moving toward truth is good.
2. **Evil** — moving away from truth is evil. This concept didn't really exist before in the same way. Previously, you did good things because it was advantageous. Now, defying God is evil.
3. **The Individual** — before, what mattered was family, community, society. Now, your personal loyalty and faith in the one true God is what matters. You can step outside your community and act on individual conscience.

> "These three concepts, truth, evil, individual, are important for you to remember because they underpin modernity."

**Key claim**: These three concepts are **counterintuitive** — you cannot logically deduce them by yourself. The mother goddess and polytheistic worlds are intuitive. The monotheistic world is counterintuitive. Because of this, monotheism had to **destroy** the polytheistic world to triumph.

### From Mystery Schools to Secret Societies

#### Main Idea

This is the origin story of secret societies. The lecturer traces a direct line from fertility cults to mystery schools to secret societies.

**The sequence**:

1. In the mother goddess civilization, **fertility cults** run by women were the main instrument of worship
2. When war came and men took over, women went **underground** to create **mystery schools**
3. Mystery schools tried to maintain the best knowledge and traditions of the mother goddess civilization — especially the idea that mind leads to matter, that we created the universe through imagination
4. Mystery schools became popular among the elite because they offered elaborate sexual rituals — secret techniques to please women and achieve harmony with the universe (sex as a mechanism to communicate with the universe)
5. They were called "mystery schools" not because they were secret, but because members were **sworn to secrecy** — divulging what happened meant death
6. With the advent of Christianity, mystery schools became threats to the empire because they were where local elites gathered
7. Mystery schools then became **secret societies**

> "Secret societies are an attempt to maintain the secrets of the universe against the advent of Christianity and empire."

Well-known examples: Knights Templars, Rosicrucians, the Illuminati, the Freemasons. The lecturer emphasizes there are many more we don't know about, and they've been around much longer.

**The great secret they protect**: Mind leads to matter.

### The Orthodox/Canonical Christian Narrative: Six Covenants

#### Main Idea

The lecturer lays out the official biblical narrative as a series of **covenants** (contracts/understandings) between God and humans. This is presented as necessary context before revealing the esoteric interpretation.

The six covenants:

1. **Adam** — God creates paradise (Garden of Eden). Only rule: don't eat from the tree of knowledge of good and evil. Adam and Eve eat the fruit. God banishes them.
2. **Noah** — Humans become wicked. God decides to destroy the world but saves Noah. After the flood, God promises never to destroy the world again.
3. **Abraham** — God finds a good man, makes him father of Israel. The Israelites are the "chosen people" given the "promised land."
4. **Moses** — The Israelites become slaves in Egypt. God appoints Moses to free them. God gives the Ten Commandments (the Law) because humans can't be trusted to behave.
5. **David** — God finds his ideal leader. The **Davidic covenant** states God will always favor Israel, and the house of David will rule forever. David and his son Solomon represent the golden age of Israel.
6. **Jesus** — Reasserts the law of Moses. Sacrifices himself to free humanity from original sin. Creates a new covenant: anyone can now love God, not just Israelites. This starts Christianity.

**Editorial aside from the lecturer**: He asks, "Who wrote the Bible?" and suggests it was probably David — since the narrative positions David as God's ultimate favorite whose family will rule forever.

**Judaism vs. Christianity**: Jews believe the first five covenants. They believe someone will return to restore David's kingdom, but not Jesus. Christians believe Jesus is the final prophet — God himself.

### Three Major Problems with the Orthodox Narrative

#### Main Idea

The lecturer identifies three questions that trouble thoughtful believers:

1. **The Garden of Eden** — Why was eating a fruit so terrible that it warranted banishment from paradise? What was really wrong with the fruit?
2. **Noah** — God destroyed the world because humans were wicked, but after the flood, humans were still wicked. If God is omniscient, shouldn't he have known this wouldn't solve anything?
3. **Jesus** — Who was Jesus, and why did God need to send his own son to die? The logic of the sacrifice doesn't make intuitive sense.

> "I grew up in the West. I grew up in a Christian world, and it never made sense to me."

### The Nephilim

#### Main Idea

The reason God destroyed the world wasn't because of human wickedness — it was because of the **Nephilim**. The Nephilim are children of angels and human women.

**What happened**: Angels assigned to watch humanity came down and had sex with human women. Their children — the Nephilim — enslaved humanity and then began fighting among themselves.

#### Supporting Material: The Avengers Thought Experiment

The lecturer uses a pop culture analogy: imagine the Avengers (Spider-Man, Iron Man, Thor) save the world, then get bored, have lots of children, and those children become demagogues who enslave humanity and fight each other. That's essentially the Nephilim story.

**The problem**: God destroyed the Nephilim's mortal bodies, but because they are partly divine, they cannot truly be killed. They became **demons** living underground and in shadows.

**Key claim from secret societies**: The Nephilim are still here today. The richest people in the world are actually Nephilim who have been accumulating power for thousands of years.

### The Cosmology of the Secret Societies: Mind Leads to Matter

#### Main Idea

The lecturer now explains the full cosmological system that secret societies believe. This is the heart of the lecture.

**The creation sequence**:

1. **The Monad** ("The One") — the true god, the core of the universe, like a divine sun. So powerful that when it breathes, it vibrates divine energy.
2. Over millions of years, this energy creates **Diads** — pairs (usually male/female) that are children of the Monad. Diads can create new universes by emanating energy.
3. Through continued emanation, vibrations eventually become **atoms**, which become **matter**.

> "The entire universe is vibration. It's vibrational energy. It's the breathing of these cosmic beings."

**This is what "mind leads to matter" means.**

#### The Fall: Sophia and the Demiurge

One of the Diads, named **Sophia**, decides she can reproduce alone like the Monad (without mating). She creates a being called the **Demiurge** — a monstrosity, an abomination against natural order.

Sophia abandons the Demiurge, hiding him in a sea of clouds. The Demiurge doesn't know the true universe exists. He believes he is the one true God. So what does he create? **Us. Earth. Adam and Eve.**

#### The Demiurge as the God of the Bible

The lecturer connects each troubling aspect of the biblical narrative to the Demiurge being a monster:

- What kind of god kicks someone out of paradise for eating a fruit? **A monster.**
- What kind of god kills people for no reason? **A monster.**
- What kind of god demands unconditional loyalty? **A monster.**
- What kind of god plays favorites? **A monster.**

> "The true secret that these secret societies are trying to keep is the god we worship today is the false god. And we are actually in a prison created by this false god."

#### Who Was Jesus?

In this framework, **Jesus was a cosmic being sent by the Monad** to tell humanity the truth — that we live in a prison created by a false god. He was killed because the Roman Empire couldn't allow people to see the truth.

> "If I told you the truth, you would know it right away. You would wake up. Therefore, the truth tellers must be killed."

#### The Divine Spark and the Path Back

Even though we're in a prison, the Monad exists within each person as a **divine spark** — a light that can be activated. If activated, your soul can escape this world and return to the Monad when you die.

**How to activate it**:

- Be a good person
- Love one another
- Reject money, materialism, competition, grades
- Pursue spiritual happiness over material wealth

**Opposition**: The Nephilim and the Demiurge both oppose this message because it threatens their control over the prison world. This is why secret societies have been persecuted, executed, and massacred throughout history.

#### Why Secret Societies Remain Secret

Two reasons:

1. They believe most people **cannot accept the truth** — you'd think they were crazy and report them
2. They wait for people to **wake up on their own** and approach them. Then they test you and reveal the secrets.

### Paradise Lost as a Secret Society Text

#### Main Idea

The lecturer argues that secret societies embedded their secrets in poetry and literature. The greatest example is **Paradise Lost** by **John Milton**, described as the greatest poem in the English language and the national epic of the British Empire.

**About Milton**:

- A rebel and free thinker
- Passionate advocate of free speech
- Wrote *Paradise Lost* while **blind** — he spoke it aloud and secretaries wrote it down
- Was a member of secret societies
- The fact that a blind man could recite such poetry suggests he is a prophet, a messenger revealing divine truth

**About the poem's status**:

- Foundational text of many secret societies
- Elite members of the British Empire memorized it
- Contains the secrets of the universe, visible only to those who already understand those secrets

**The plot**: Milton reimagines the Fall of Man. Satan, God's favorite angel, rebels, loses the war, and is banished to hell with the other devils. The devils debate what to do. Satan decides to go to the Garden of Eden to corrupt Adam and Eve.

### Satan's First Speech: The Hero's Journey

#### Main Idea

Satan volunteers to escape hell alone and corrupt humanity. No other devil wants to go because the journey is incredibly dangerous — escaping hell, crossing the abyss of space, finding Earth.

Key passage (paraphrased): "I am your leader. Therefore I must go. You trusted me to lead you, and I will risk everything to prove my worth. I will go alone."

> "This enterprise, none shall partake with me."

#### Esoteric Interpretations of This Speech

The lecturer identifies three layers of meaning that secret societies find in this speech:

1. **The soul's ascent** — Satan's escape from hell mirrors our souls leaving our bodies to reach the heavens. Some secret societies believe that when you die, the first person you meet is Satan, because he knows how to navigate from the prison world to the light.

2. **Birth imagery** — the escape from hell resembles a baby leaving the womb. The prison of hell = the womb. Escape = birth.

3. **Life and death are the same** — to be born, you must die. When you die, you are born again.

4. **Initiation ceremony** — secret societies use this speech during initiation. When you join, you must choose to fight for all humanity alone. You must accept that people will hunt you for speaking the truth.

### Satan's Second Speech: The Temptation of Eve

#### Main Idea

This is identified as the more important speech. The serpent (Satan) convinces Eve to eat the fruit from the tree of knowledge. The lecturer reads the passage, then makes a provocative argument.

**Satan's argument to Eve** (summarized):

1. God said you'd die if you eat the fruit — but look at me, I ate it and I'm still alive
2. A true God would **reward** risk-taking and the pursuit of knowledge, not punish it
3. You can only be good if you know what evil is — ignorance isn't virtue
4. Why would God forbid this? Either to test you or to keep you ignorant
5. If God controls everything, how could eating a fruit possibly threaten him — unless he's a false god?

> "God therefore cannot hurt you and be just. Not just, not God."

#### The Conventional Academic Reading vs. the Esoteric Reading

At Yale, the lecturer was taught that this speech demonstrates Satan's cleverness and evil — he's lying to Eve, God is omniscient, and Eve is foolish for believing him.

**But** — and this is the lecture's pivotal claim — when you actually read the Bible, **Satan is telling the truth.**

### Reading Genesis: Satan Was Telling the Truth

#### Main Idea

The lecturer reads directly from Genesis to support the claim that Satan's words align with what the Bible actually says.

**Evidence from the text**:

1. The serpent says "you will not die, for God knows that when you eat of it, your eyes will be opened, and you'll be like God, knowing good and evil" — this matches what happens
2. After eating the fruit: "Then the eyes of both were opened" — **Satan told the truth**
3. The punishment for disobedience was pain in childbirth (Eve) and hard labor (Adam) — **not** banishment
4. The reason for banishment is different: "See, the man has become like one of us, knowing good and evil, and now he might reach out his hand and take also from the tree of life and eat and live forever"

**Key revelation**: There are **two trees** — the tree of knowledge (gives you the mind of God) and the tree of life (lets you live forever). God banished Adam and Eve because if they ate from both trees, they would become like God.

> "Satan was telling the truth, and God was lying to Adam and Eve all along."

#### How Did Eve Know Satan Was Telling the Truth?

A student asks this. The lecturer's answer: **the divine light within her** — the spark of the Monad that connects every person to the true God. The false God lies, but inside you, in your heart, the light tells you what is true.

### The Nephilim in Genesis

#### Main Idea

The lecturer reads from Genesis to show the Nephilim in the canonical text:

> "The Nephilim were on the earth in those days... these were the heroes that were of old, warriors of renown."

**Two important implications**:

1. The "heroes of old, warriors of renown" are figures from other mythologies — Hercules, Achilles, Theseus. They are recast as Nephilim.
2. The "sons of God" are the old gods — Zeus, Ares, Apollo — now demoted to mere angels in the monotheistic framework.

**Connection**: The Bible is presented as a "tremendous act of propaganda" that reinvents reality around one true God, absorbing and subordinating all prior mythologies. In doing so, it denies human intuition and historical memory.

> "The Bible is a tremendous act of propaganda that tries to reinvent reality around one true God."

### Why the Monad Doesn't Intervene

#### Main Idea

A student asks: if the Monad is the true God, why doesn't it stop the Demiurge? The lecturer offers two explanations:

**Explanation 1**: The Monad doesn't interfere in material affairs because it is pure mind. We have the capacity to reject this world and return to the Monad through our own free will. Forcing us would contradict the principle of choice.

**Explanation 2** (attributed to Dante): The Monad is eternal, perfect, and immutable — and therefore **has no imagination**. Growth requires imagination. The Monad allowed the Demiurge to create humans because our flaws, mistakes, and evil **expand the imagination of the universe**, which allows the Monad to grow.

> "Each time we imagine, we return to the Monad."

**Key paradox**: The Monad is eternal but also becoming. Our imagination feeds it. Evil and good must coexist — you can only become good by first doing evil, because evil gives you the chance to become better.

### The Ultimate Secret

#### Main Idea

A student asks: when we pray, are we praying to the true God or the false God?

The lecturer's answer, according to secret societies: **you're praying to the false God.** The true God is inside you.

> "Don't pray to an outside force. Look inside you and ask yourself, I know for a fact what is true. If I believe in myself enough, then I will find the truth."

This is identified as a recurring theme throughout western poetry and literature:

- We will always be in conflict between good and evil
- The answer is not to surrender to an external God but to **believe in yourself**
- Mistakes become your strength because they become your wisdom
- The Monad rewards self-belief
- Believing in yourself often means **defying authority, making mistakes, transgressing against rules**

> "The possibility for redemption, for growth, for imagination is all in us because of our connection to the Monad."

### Key Takeaways

- Secret societies originated as mystery schools preserving mother goddess traditions against the rise of monotheism and empire
- The central secret they protect: mind leads to matter — consciousness creates reality, not the other way around
- The esoteric reading of the Bible reframes the God of scripture as the Demiurge (a false god/prison warden) and Satan as a truth-teller
- *Paradise Lost* by John Milton is identified as a foundational secret society text with embedded esoteric knowledge
- The divine spark (connection to the Monad) exists in every person and is activated through self-belief, not external worship
- The lecture previews future topics: how secret societies became corrupted over time

### Key Terms Introduced

- **Monad**: "The One" — the true god at the core of the universe whose breathing/vibration creates all existence
- **Demiurge**: A monstrous being created by Sophia in violation of natural order; believed to be the false god of the Bible
- **Nephilim**: Children of angels and human women; believed by secret societies to still control the world today
- **Diads**: Pairs (male/female) emanated by the Monad; capable of creating new universes
- **Sophia**: One of the Diads who attempted to reproduce alone, accidentally creating the Demiurge
- **Covenant**: A contract or understanding between God and humans; the Bible contains six major ones
- **Fertility Cults**: Communal sexual rituals in mother goddess civilization; the origin point of mystery schools
- **Mystery Schools**: Underground religious organizations created by women to preserve mother goddess knowledge; precursors to secret societies
- **Apocryphal**: Hidden, secret, or esoteric knowledge kept within secret societies
- **Pantheon**: A hierarchy of gods created through the outcomes of wars between societies
- **Davidic Covenant**: God's promise that the house of David will rule Israel forever
