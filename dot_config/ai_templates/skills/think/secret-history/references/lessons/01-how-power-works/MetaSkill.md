# Secret History #1 — How Power Works

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lt8XLz78ZvY
**Series:** Predictive History — Secret History

## Overview

Opening lecture of a semester-long course. The professor establishes three core illusions — money, the individual, and the nation state — as constructs engineered to control human behavior. Starting from Kant's noumena/phenomena distinction, he argues we never access objective reality, so every "reality" we live inside is imagined and therefore manipulable.

Through a bank thought experiment, he shows money is created from nothing and poverty is deliberately maintained so money feels valuable. He then argues the modern individual is a historically recent invention that atomizes people and blocks collective action, and that mandatory schooling (Sparta, Aztecs, Prussia) exists to separate children from parents and implant the nation state. All three constructs, he claims, descend from monotheism and function as power's alchemy — turning nothing into everything.

## Key themes

- Kant's noumena vs. phenomena as the course's epistemological foundation
- Bank thought experiment: $5M deposit + $5M loan = $9.5M, fractional reserves, receipts-as-gold
- Bank runs, sovereign default, and the rise of central banking cartels via intermarriage
- Poverty as deliberately manufactured to make money feel valuable ("artificial misery")
- Polytheism (eudaimonia, chaotic gods) vs. scientific individualism (atomization, psychiatry)
- Mandatory schooling in Sparta, the Aztecs, and Prussia as war-making institutions
- School as a device to separate children from parents and implant nation-state belief
- Monotheism as the common origin of money, the individual, and the nation state
- Power defined as alchemy — the capacity to turn nothing into everything
- World of Warcraft and the feast tradition as intuition pumps for the argument
