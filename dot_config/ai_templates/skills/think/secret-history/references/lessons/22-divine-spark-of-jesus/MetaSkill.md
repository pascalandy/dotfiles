# Secret History #22 — The Divine Spark of Jesus

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pp0E1gb80WQ
**Series:** Predictive History — Secret History

## Overview

Separates the historical Jesus from the Catholic Jesus. Scholarship confirms only a few facts: born ~4 BCE in Galilee, taught by John the Baptist, crucified by the Romans (probably as a rebel), succeeded by his brother James the Just leading the Ebionites. Canonical narratives (four contradictory Gospels, Sadducees/Pharisees/Essenes politics) are read as Roman-shaped propaganda blaming Jews for a Roman execution — a charge the lecturer dismantles with three arguments (Jewish pluralism, the taboo against handing Jews to Rome, James living unharmed in Jerusalem).

The Gospel of Thomas (Nag Hammadi, 1945) is presented as Jesus' real teaching, aligned with Homer, Plato, Zoroaster, Buddhism, and later Dante, Nietzsche, Rumi, Dostoevsky: the monad emits a divine spark into every person; the world is a Roman corpse that binds the evil and frees the compassionate; forgiving and loving your enemy is how you release them. Saying after saying is unpacked — "the kingdom is inside of you," "I found them intoxicated," the Pharisees hiding the keys, lost-sheep and dinner-party parables, Sermon on the Mount reread, "do not say 'Lord, Lord'" — and Dostoevsky's Grand Inquisitor kiss plus Dante's Beatrice, daughter-and-dog analogy, and rejection of ransom theory round out why Jesus had to die.

## Key themes

- Historical Jesus: Galilee ~4 BCE, John the Baptist, Romans crucified him, James the Just and the Ebionites
- Three Jewish groups (Sadducees, Pharisees, Essenes) and three arguments against Jewish culpability
- Nag Hammadi 1945 and the Gospel of Thomas as Jesus' genuine voice
- Divine spark cosmology shared with Homer, Plato, Zoroaster, Rumi, Nietzsche, Dante
- "The kingdom is inside of you" and Jesus not being a Christian
- The world as a corpse: Rome wins by being evil and loses everything
- Love and forgive your enemy as the way to release them — Priam/Achilles dynamic scaled up
- Parables: lost sheep, great dinner party, serpents and doves, Pharisees hiding the keys
- Sermon on the Mount as surviving Jesus-voice inside the canon; "do not say Lord, Lord"
- Dostoevsky's Grand Inquisitor kiss and Dante's free-will justification (daughter and dog analogy)
