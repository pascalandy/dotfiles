# Secret History #7 — Death by Meritocracy

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JU_8fJjtGxA
**Series:** Predictive History — Secret History

## Overview

A history and critique of the American meritocracy. The lecturer traces the Ivy League from Puritan theocracy (Harvard training ministers) through post-industrial social clubs, the rise of A&M state schools and German-style research universities (Chicago, Johns Hopkins), and Harvard's counterattack via James Conant and Henry Chauncey — who built the SAT and ETS to reclaim dominance, then installed "holistic" character admissions to keep Jews (and later Asians) out.

Using his own Yale file, the lecturer argues elite admissions behave like a venture capital firm optimizing for high-variance graduates with dissociative identity profiles — desperate, insecure, transgressive. The system both selects for trauma and manufactures it, destroys real learning (open-mindedness, failure, reflection, resilience), and concentrates power in a handful of schools whose alumni dominate generals, CEOs, judges, and senators. Obama, Larry Summers, Tim Geithner, Vance, Johnny Kim, and Skull and Bones anchor the political case study.

## Key themes

- Ivy League as Puritan theocracy → social club → threatened elite institution
- A&M state schools and German-modeled research universities (Chicago, Johns Hopkins)
- SAT originally built as Harvard's scholarship recruiting weapon
- Holistic "character" admissions engineered to exclude Jews, then limit Asians
- Secrecy and discretion as Harvard's governing principles vs. Gaokao transparency
- Admissions as venture capital: prefer high-variance bets over reliable successes
- Dissociative identity profile (desperation + insecurity + transgression) as the target trait
- Three-level trauma factory: Yale Hunger Games, high school prep, conditional-love parenting
- Architects Conant and Chauncey, ETS, and the Nature study of Harvard elite dominance
- Obama, Summers, Geithner, moral hazard, Vance, Johnny Kim, Skull and Bones as outputs
