# Secret History #19 — Dawn of the Jews

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3t0MeVBD24I
**Series:** Predictive History — Secret History

## Overview

Introduces the lecturer's "predictive history" framework — historical patterns, game theory, and religious eschatology — then applies it to the Persian period. Three geopolitical laws (internal elite competition > inter-state, elites have no loyalty, war as equilibrium) are illustrated with the Warring States' Qin victory (a total-war outsider breaking ritualized rules, exposing Sun Tzu as a manual for cheating within equilibrium) and Lugal-zage-si breaking the Mesopotamian temple taboo.

The central thesis: Jewish identity as we know it was engineered by the Persian Empire as a divide-and-rule instrument for the Levant. Cyrus the Great's 539 BCE Edict and Artaxerxes' letter to Ezra are reread as imperial strategy; Ezra and Nehemiah rebuild Jerusalem, enforce blood purity (no intermarriage, Sabbath, interest-free loans within the group), and make the Torah the Jews' portable national memory. The Balfour Declaration is presented as the same move by the British Empire. The lecture closes with three predictions — Third Temple replacing Al-Aqsa, Israeli theocracy, and a US–Israel divorce inaugurating Pax Judaica — and insists on distinguishing Israelites, Jews, and Israelis.

## Key themes

- Predictive history = historical patterns + game theory + religious eschatology
- Three geopolitical laws: internal elite competition dominates; elites have no loyalty; war as equilibrium
- Qin's total war and the critique of Sun Tzu as cheating inside ritualized warfare
- Lugal-zage-si breaking the Mesopotamian temple-taboo and Sargon of Akkad's outsider ascent
- Levant as the strategic center of the world; controlling it threatens Egypt and trade
- Persian innovations: Zoroastrian-fueled honest bureaucracy, divide-and-rule satraps, first postal system
- Cyrus's Edict (539 BCE) as imperial engineering of a loyal Levantine client group ("Jews")
- Ezra / Nehemiah enforce blood purity, Sabbath, no intermarriage, and interest-free internal lending
- Torah as portable national memory — the source of diaspora resilience
- Three predictions: Third Temple, Israeli theocracy, US–Israel divorce and Pax Judaica
