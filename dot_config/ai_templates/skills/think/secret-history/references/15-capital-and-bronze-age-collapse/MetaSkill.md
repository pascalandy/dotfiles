# Secret History #15 — Capital and the Bronze Age Collapse

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rkt0HQOogHE
**Series:** Predictive History — Secret History

## Overview

Traces how bronze, an alloy needing scattered tin, forced the ancient world into globalization and became its first universal currency. Capital is defined by three traits — universality, store of value, mobility — and bronze (like today's US dollar) is the first commodity to clear all three. Capital flips humans from altruistic into utilitarian mode, producing a repeatable cycle of innovation, exploitation, consolidation, and collapse.

Around 1200 BCE that system breaks: drought, earthquakes, migration, and the Sea Peoples bankrupt Egypt, destroy Mycenaean Greece and the Hittite Empire, and overwhelm the Indus Valley; Mesopotamia survives because it was always contested. The lecturer reads Eric Cline's "perfect storm" through capital's internal logic, brings in David Graeber's Debt (money as symbol of unpayable blood, marriage, and funerary obligations), and argues empires are really trade routes — Troy the chokepoint of its era. The lecture closes with a neurological argument (power as brain damage, Brian Johnson, the sunk-cost Satan bargain) and the Bronze Age collapse as the birth canal of the Greek polis and the alphabet.

## Key themes

- Bronze as first universal capital; tin's scarcity as the engine of ancient globalization
- Three criteria of capital (universal, store of value, mobile) applied to grain, cattle, slaves, bronze, dollars
- Capital flips humans from altruistic to utilitarian mode and guarantees a collapse cycle
- 1200 BCE perfect storm: drought, earthquakes, Sea Peoples, revolutions, mass migration
- Mesopotamia's constant competition as the reason it outlasts Egypt, Hittites, Mycenaeans, Indus
- Indus Valley's peaceful rejection of bronze-as-weapon; BMAC tin routes into Iran and India
- David Graeber's Debt: money as marker of unpayable blood, marriage, and funerary debts
- Empires are trade routes — Troy as the Bronze Age chokepoint and target
- Mycenaean palace economy, Lion's Gate, Agamemnon-era tombs as warrior-aristocracy culture
- Power-as-brain-damage, Brian Johnson, and the sunk-cost Satan bargain for the ultra-wealthy
