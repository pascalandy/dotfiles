## Jewish History: From the Second Temple to Sabbatian Frankism

### Overview

This is a university lecture covering Jewish history from the Persian period through the emergence of Sabbatian Frankism in the 17th-18th centuries. The lecture traces how geopolitical pressures shaped Jewish identity, how repeated persecution created a crisis of faith, and how messianic figures—particularly Sabbatai Zevi and Jacob Frank—offered radical philosophical alternatives to rabbinic Judaism. The bulk of the lecture is devoted to close reading of Jacob Frank's recorded teachings.

---

### The Strategic Importance of Jerusalem

#### Main Idea

For most of human history, the Middle East was the wealthiest region in the world. Jerusalem, situated in the Levant, functioned as the geographic nexus connecting Egypt, Anatolia, Mesopotamia, Persia, and the Mediterranean. Whoever controlled Jerusalem controlled the most profitable trade access point of any empire.

#### Supporting Material: The Problem of Loyalty

Because of its strategic position, Jerusalem could always be bribed to switch allegiances. If Mesopotamia controlled it, Egypt would offer incentives to flip sides, and vice versa. Empires tried various techniques to maintain control:

- **Babylonians**: Took captives and hostages—didn't work well, so they destroyed Jerusalem entirely, splintering the identity of its people
- **Persians**: Conquered the Babylonians and adopted a new technique—**divide and rule**. They created and implanted the Jewish identity in Jerusalem to generate conflict with locals. Jews would remain loyal to Persia as a foreign entity dependent on Persian backing. In return, Persia financed the construction of the **Second Temple**

**Connection**: The Jewish identity emerges not in a vacuum but as a geopolitical instrument. This framing sets up the lecture's recurring theme: Jewish identity shaped by external imperial pressures.

### The Three Pillars of Jewish Identity

#### Main Idea

When the Jewish identity crystallizes under Persian sponsorship, it rests on three foundational pillars that persist to this day:

1. **Monotheism and Chosenness**: Yahweh is the only god. Jews are his chosen people through a covenant with Abraham. Yahweh promised Abraham's descendants the **Promised Land**—extending from the Nile to the Euphrates

2. **The Law of Moses**: Jews must follow the Law of Moses (the Ten Commandments, understood as the law of God). Over time this law divides into:
   - **Torah**: The written law (the first five books of the Bible)
   - **Talmud**: The oral law

3. **The Bible as Collective Memory**: Jews treat the Bible as literal history. Believing in the Bible's literal truth is what makes someone Jewish—which is why Jewish identity is described as "very flexible"

#### Early Characteristics of Jewish Worship

- The **Second Temple** in Jerusalem is the center of worship—the house of Yahweh
- **Animal sacrifices** are required
- The **Sadducees** (hereditary priests) control the religion
- Strict behavioral laws: Sabbath rest, prohibition on interfaith marriage, circumcision

### The Hellenistic Period and the Maccabean Revolt

#### Main Idea

Jews expanded across the world during the Hellenistic period—working as mercenaries in Alexander the Great's army, as officials, artisans, and merchants. Three major cultures—Jewish, Greek, and Persian—merged in a process the lecturer calls **synchronization**, which gave birth to Western civilization.

Conflict arose because both Jews and Greeks were culturally chauvinistic. Many Jews converted to the Hellenistic lifestyle, but a minority of religiously committed Jews launched the **Maccabean Revolt** against the Greeks, who wanted to turn the Second Temple into a site of Greek worship.

> Despite being a minority, and despite most Jews not supporting them, the Maccabees triumphed through religious conviction.

This victory is celebrated today as **Hanukkah**. The resulting Maccabean kingdom did not last long—Jerusalem's position as a nexus of empires meant the Romans would inevitably take control.

### The Roman Period and the Destruction of the Second Temple

#### Main Idea

The Romans were more discriminatory toward the Jews than the Greeks. Three major wars broke out between the Jewish diaspora and Rome. In **70 CE**, the Romans burned down the Second Temple to destroy Jewish morale.

#### The Consequences of the Temple's Destruction

The destruction caused a fundamental shift in the Jewish religion:

- **Many Jews converted to Christianity**. The lecturer references Paul's creation of Christianity as a mechanism for Jews to remain culturally Jewish while also being Roman
- **Those who stayed became more fanatical**, developing a **messianic, eschatological attitude**—the end of the world is coming, God will destroy the Romans, and Jews must demonstrate loyalty
- These fanatical Jews retreated to the Arabian desert, where they **incubated Islam** and prophesied the coming of the Messiah—who, according to the lecturer, turned out to be Mohammed
- The religion shifted from **temple worship to worship of the law**. **Rabbis** (the Pharisees, teachers of the oral law) replaced the **Sadducees** as the religious elite

#### The Al-Aqsa Mosque as the Third Temple

When Islam conquered Jerusalem, Jews who believed the messianic age had arrived saw the **Al-Aqsa Mosque** as the **Third Temple**. Many Jews converted to Islam, believing Mohammed was the Messiah. Over time, the site became a center of Islamic worship.

### Jewish Life in Medieval Europe

#### Main Idea

As Europe Christianized, Jews were allowed to stay but faced cycles of persecution, massacre, and expulsion. The lecturer references a map showing numerous cities where at least one Jewish expulsion occurred.

#### The Implicit Contract with European Nobility

Jews entered an unwritten arrangement with European nobility:

- **Jews provided economic services the Christian elite couldn't perform themselves**:
  - **Banking and moneylending** (Christianity prohibited charging interest)
  - **Property management** and industry
  - **Merchant networks** through the Jewish diaspora
  - **War financing**
  - **Medicine** and other professional services

- **Jews served as a scapegoat**:
  - The Catholic Church kept Jews around as a warning: "If you don't believe in Jesus, you end up like the Jews"
  - When the economy turned bad, Jews were blamed—as loan sharks, as killers of Jesus, as sources of "spiritual pollution"

> "You're allowed to stay and make a lot of money, but when we need a scapegoat, you get blamed and you have to leave."

**Connection**: This arrangement explains the recurring pattern of Jewish prosperity followed by sudden expulsion or massacre throughout European history.

### Al-Andalus and the Spanish Expulsion

#### Main Idea

**Muslim Spain (Al-Andalus)** was the one place in Europe friendly toward Jews, and also the wealthiest part of the continent. The key difference: Muslims viewed Jews, Christians, and Muslims as one family—all descendants of Abraham—unlike Catholics who blamed Jews for killing Jesus.

Jews rose to high positions in Al-Andalus due to their literacy, biblical knowledge, diaspora connections, and financial expertise. This was a period when Jews, Christians, and Muslims coexisted well.

#### The Trauma of 1492

When Christians reconquered Spain, they persecuted Jews and forced conversion. In **1492**, the Spanish expelled the Jews: convert or leave.

- Many converted; many fled to the Ottoman Empire
- This was traumatic—Jews had been deeply established in Spain and considered themselves Spanish
- The event caused a **schism**: those who converted and those who refused became bitter enemies

---

### The Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth

Jews who left settled in the **Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth**, a new nation with tolerant religious policies. Jews thrived once again. But the region was geographically vulnerable—invasions from Sweden, Russia, and others brought war, and Jews were again killed in large numbers.

> "When they think they finally found a home, they have to suffer this persecution."

### The Crisis of Faith

#### Main Idea

Centuries of persecution, conversion, and displacement created three fundamental theological questions:

1. **The problem of false idols**: The first commandment forbids worshipping false idols. Why can Jews convert to Christianity and Islam without consequences? Why should loyal Jews not convert?

2. **The problem of undeserved suffering**: If some Jews sinned by converting, punish them. But why are Jews loyal to Yahweh also being persecuted and massacred?

3. **The problem of the Promised Land**: Yahweh promised a homeland. Jews have remained faithful for centuries despite everything. Why don't they have it?

**Connection**: This crisis of faith set the stage for messianic expectations. In Jewish belief, crisis signals the end times—the Messiah is coming.

### Sabbatai Zevi: The Jewish Messiah

#### Main Idea

Around 1600, anticipation of the Messiah reached a peak. **Sabbatai Zevi**, born in the Ottoman Empire, declared himself the Messiah. He is described as the Jewish equivalent of Jesus.

_[Note: The lecturer acknowledges that most information about Zevi comes from his enemies, the rabbis, and that his followers were secretive.]_

#### Zevi's Revolutionary Message

As a messianic figure, Zevi was fundamentally a revolutionary trying to overthrow the social order. His diagnosis of Jewish suffering:

- Jews are persecuted because they aren't practicing the true faith
- The **rabbis** are a corrupt elite—like the Catholic Church hierarchy
- Rabbis don't suffer the same persecution as ordinary Jews
- Rabbis help European elites control and persecute Jews through unfair agreements
- The real problem is **hierarchy within the religion** and **laws that don't make sense**

His prescription:

- **Egalitarian society**: Men and women are equal; no hierarchy; rabbis have no authority over anyone
- What matters is your **relationship to God**, not obedience to law
- The system of rabbinic authority and waiting for the Messiah is designed to **defend the status quo** and keep Jews enslaved to nobility

> The lecturer describes him as "very much pro-liberal and pro-communist."

#### Zevi's Conversion to Islam

When Zevi confronted the Ottoman Sultan, he was given an ultimatum: the turban (convert to Islam) or lose his head. He converted.

**The theological justification**: Millions of Jews had converted to Christianity and Islam over the centuries. According to the rabbis, these converts would burn in hell. But if all Jews are one family with the divine spark, they must be redeemed. Zevi's conversion was a **sacrifice**—like Jesus on the cross—redeeming all Jews from the "original sin of conversion."

By converting, Zevi declared:
- It's acceptable to convert
- He has absolved Jews of the sin of conversion
- The laws no longer need to be followed
- **The messianic age has arrived**—"you are the messianic age now"

#### Justification by Sin

Zevi introduced the concept of **justification by sin**: in the messianic age, laws don't apply because everyone has the internal wisdom to follow their own moral compass. Breaking the laws demonstrates true faith in the Messiah and in God—freedom from rabbinic authority.

_[The lecturer notes there has been significant misinterpretation of this concept.]_

#### The Aftermath

- Most followers chose not to follow Zevi after his conversion, returning to the rabbis
- A few families did convert with him—these became known as the **Dönme**
- The lecturer claims the Dönme eventually came to control Turkey, citing **Mustafa Kemal Atatürk** as a Dönme member

### Jacob Frank: The Reincarnation of Sabbatai Zevi

#### Main Idea

Jews believe in reincarnation. Fifty years after Zevi's death, **Jacob Frank** emerged, born into a **Sabbatean family**, considering himself Zevi's reincarnation. He systematized and extended Zevi's teachings.

Frank became very successful—gaining followers, befriending European royalty (Austria and Russia), and producing followers who became prominent intellectuals, members of the nobility, and bankers.

#### Sabbatian Frankism: A New Philosophy

Frank's religion—**Sabbatian Frankism**—is explicitly **not Judaism**. Frank hated Jews. His philosophy:

- **Jesus taught that the material world is a corpse**—what matters is the divine spark within
- Frank's extension: If God is forgiving and wants us to learn wisdom, **why must we suffer?** Why not become kings? Why not hack the system?
- He taught his followers **how to master and conquer the material world**

The lecturer compares Frank's teachings to Machiavelli's _The Prince_—a guide to conquering the world through stories.

> "Stories are the most powerful mechanisms of education in the world."

### Close Reading of Frank's Teachings

_The following are translated from Polish by Harris Lenowitz from the University of Utah. The lecturer emphasizes these are Frank's public teachings only—his secret Kabbalistic/mystical teachings remain inaccessible._

#### Story 1: The Knife on the Sabbath

Frank peels oranges with a penknife on the Sabbath. A learned Jewish scholar scolds him. Frank asks the scholar to check his own pocket—and the scholar finds a knife there (Frank had slipped it in through sleight of hand). The scholar is so amazed he declares Frank must be divine.

**Lessons embedded in the story**:
- Contempt for learned Jews—their strict adherence to law makes them stupid and easily manipulated
- You control people by **using their own laws against them**
- Simple tricks work because people are superstitious and ignorant

**Connection**: The story teaches followers that people are fundamentally susceptible to manipulation through their own belief systems.

#### Story 2: The Plague Town

Frank sends his companion to buy goods from plague-stricken sellers. Each seller dies of plague before receiving payment. When a horseman knocks the companion down, the horseman also dies.

**Literal interpretation**: When people are afraid, take advantage of their fear. Where others see plague, see opportunity.

**Spiritual interpretation**: If you see the world as a corpse (as Jesus taught), you stand above people. You can take whatever you want from those who are, spiritually speaking, already dead.

**Connection**: This illustrates **justification by sin**—breaking the laws of man proves true faith in God. With true faith, you can do whatever you want and triumph.

#### Story 3: Why Didn't Jesus Become King?

Frank challenges rabbis: if divine beings come to experience everything, why did Jesus only experience suffering? Why didn't he also experience being a king?

**Connection**: Laws, morality, and religion are mechanisms of control and subjugation. Frank's religion is explicitly a **religion of liberation from oppression**.

#### Story 4: The Door No One Could Open

Frank claims that the patriarchs—Moses, Aaron, David, Solomon—all tried to open a metaphorical door and failed. They were slaves who couldn't see the true structure of the world. Frank claims he can open it and will show his followers the way.

**Connection**: His followers (called **the brothers and sisters**) form a flat hierarchy—no pyramid structure. They all follow Frank, who leads them to a "promised land."

#### Story 5: Jesus's Spring Flows to Frank

In a dream, Frank sees Jesus with priests around a spring of living water. The spring flows away from Jesus and toward Frank.

> "As Christ said that he came to free the world from the hands of Satan, but I came to free it from all laws and customs."

**Connection**: Frank positions himself as superseding Jesus. The laws and customs of the world are the work of Satan. By breaking them, you reveal truth and accelerate the end of this world.

#### The Concept of Accelerationism

> "By sinning, you accelerate the end of the world. By sinning, you reveal the truth of the world. By sinning, you empower yourself."

This world is controlled by a false god (Satan). By sinning—defying Satan's laws—Frank's followers hasten the arrival of a new world where truth is revealed and evil is defeated.

#### Story 6: The Pearl and the Apprentice

A merchant has the world's most valuable pearl and wants a hole drilled through it. All expert artisans refuse—the pearl is too valuable, they might damage it. A lowly apprentice (Frank) says "sure" and does it successfully, becoming wealthy.

**Lesson**: Don't obey conventions. Don't listen to others or conform. What matters is **fearlessness**. Seize opportunity and you become powerful.

#### Story 7: The King's Four Sons

A king dies. Four sons of the same age compete for the throne by shooting an arrow at the dead king's heart. Three try and miss. The fourth refuses—"He's my father." The mystic declares the fourth son king because he understands what matters: **family**.

**Lesson**: Outsiders are treated as animals—they're stupid, governed by idiotic laws. But within the company (brothers and sisters), love and respect are paramount.

> "Our love for each other is what will allow us to conquer the world."

#### Story 8: The Bear and the Four Brothers

A bear terrorizes a village. Threats of entire districts joining forces don't scare it. But when told "we'll send four brothers against you," the bear flees.

**Lesson**: Four united brothers are more powerful than ten thousand men. The power of love—divine spark harnessed collectively—conquers the world.

#### Story 9: The Prince and the Elephant

A king offers his daughter to whoever kills a rampaging elephant. A prince, disguised as a commoner, accepts—but first asks to see the princess. The princess recognizes him and begs him not to risk his life. The prince keeps his promise and kills the elephant anyway.

**Lesson**: Do things not for money or power, but **out of love for each other**. That's the secret to conquering the world.

#### Story 10: The Sultan and the Criminal

A sultan visits a slave galley and asks each prisoner about his crime. All claim innocence. One admits everything. The sultan releases him—reasoning a criminal shouldn't be among "honest people." The released criminal pledges devoted service.

**Lesson**: Differentiate between good and bad "animals" (people). Good ones, treated well, will be loyal forever.

#### Story 11: The Circumcision Contradiction

At a Jewish wedding, Frank wins the seat of honor through disputation, not fighting. He asks: one passage says the father should circumcise the child; another says the mother should. No one can answer. He sits beside the groom.

**Lesson**: The Bible is full of contradictions. Reveal them and people submit. Win the world through **argument and learning**, not violence.

> "Go become lawyers. Go become scholars."

The lecturer notes that by the nineteenth century, all the top lawyers in Warsaw were Frankists.

#### Story 12: The Pear Garden (Rewriting Eden and Augustine)

Frank tells a story of stealing pears from a lord's walled garden. He outsmarts the traps (a pistol rigged to a cord), steals what he wants, then invokes the lord's own agreement to make peace.

**Two rewrites embedded**:
1. **Garden of Eden**: God told Adam and Eve not to eat the fruit—Frank says it's God's fault, not theirs
2. **Augustine's Confessions**: Augustine stole pears and concluded he was inherently evil. Frank says that's a stupid lesson—God wants us to break the rules and become wealthy

**Lesson**: God cares about creativity, willingness to explore, and testing boundaries. As long as you don't break fundamental laws (killing, hurting people), God won't punish you. Don't worry about morality or convention—gain power through trickery and deception.

### The Kabbalistic Worldview

#### Main Idea

Frank is a **Kabbalist**—a mystic. In the mystical Jewish tradition, the universe is consciousness and vibrations. Reality is a hallucination—what we imagine it to be. Laws, taboos, and transgressions are all invented constructs with no relevance to reality.

> "If everyone thinks these laws exist, but you know they don't exist, that makes you king."

The lecturer connects this to **imagination as the mechanism of world control**. Writers imagine worlds and readers enter them. Television creates realities viewers accept as real. Frank teaches followers to train their minds the same way—create your own reality and others will live in it.

A student asks: "If I create my own world inside my head but don't tell others, how can I get others to follow?"

The lecturer responds: this requires trained imagination—through constant education, reading, dialogue, and debate. The Frankists are powerful not because they're robbers but because they're deeply engaged in learning and exercising imagination.

### The Frankist Theology

#### The Elohim and the Purpose of Sin

The **Elohim** (gods in a hierarchy) wanted to make man as servants—imbued with spirit and flesh. The true God wanted man to be "all flesh, all pleasure"—so that man could sin. Sinning produces knowledge and wisdom that even the gods cannot access.

> "Because we can sin, we can learn knowledge in a way that the gods cannot."

Following the laws keeps you stupid. Breaking rules reveals reality.

#### The Eschatology

- The Messiah is actually Frank's daughter, **Eva**
- At the end of days, the **Big Brother** (an interdimensional higher god) will come
- Unlike other religions where the afterlife is spiritual, Frank promises **physical eternal life**—full pleasure, food, sex, drink
- Freedom from death itself

#### Three Understandings of Faith (Historical Progression)

1. **Catholics**: Justification by works—do what the Church says, avoid sin, go to heaven
2. **Martin Luther**: Justification by faith—truly believe in God to reach heaven
3. **Sabbatai Zevi / Jacob Frank**: **Justification by sin**—go against the laws of man, be shunned by your community, and that demonstrates true faith. Sinning produces knowledge, and faith grows more powerful

Frank's followers—mostly Jews—collectively converted to Catholicism to gain access to greater opportunities and patronage from Catholic royalty. Before converting, they held a private ceremony.

The ceremony involved communal nudity, kneeling before a cross, and ritualized sexual relations directed by Frank. The lecturer interprets this not as mere sex but as a **ritual of love**—cementing the covenant among followers before they dispersed.

> "No matter how far and wide you go, remember that we still love each other. And because of our love for each other, we'll find each other."

#### The Role of Women and Sexual Practices

The Frankists practiced wife swapping and incest (Frank with his daughter Eva, to create a body for reincarnation). The lecturer argues this was part of an egalitarian framework—wife swapping gave women power to meet and influence powerful men, rather than being confined to the home.

**Connection**: You must sink low to truly find God. You must destroy yourself to create yourself. Good requires evil; beauty requires ugliness. This is the Frankist attitude, and the lecturer argues it has conquered modern Western culture.

### Key Takeaways

- Jewish identity was shaped by geopolitical pressures—created as a Persian instrument of control, then forged through centuries of persecution
- The destruction of the Second Temple caused the most significant transformation: from temple worship to law-based worship, from Sadducees to rabbis
- Repeated persecution and expulsion created a crisis of faith with three unanswerable theological questions
- Sabbatai Zevi offered a radical answer: egalitarianism, liberation from rabbinic law, and justification by sin
- Jacob Frank systematized these ideas into a practical philosophy of world conquest through learning, argument, imagination, secrecy, and mutual love
- Frank taught through stories—each one encoding principles about manipulation, fearlessness, the power of love, and the illusory nature of social convention
- Frankism explicitly rejects Judaism and positions itself as a religion of material success and accelerationism
- The lecturer argues Frankist philosophy fundamentally shaped Western modernity, infiltrated major institutions, and continues to influence world events
