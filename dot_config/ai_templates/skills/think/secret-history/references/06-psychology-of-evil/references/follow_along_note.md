## Secret Societies — Mind Control as Their Secret Weapon

This lecture explores how ancient civilizations—particularly Egypt—developed techniques for programming human minds through trauma and dissociation. The instructor traces a line from Egyptian priesthood practices to modern programs like MK Ultra and the War on Terror, arguing that the same "script" for mind control has persisted for five thousand years. ### Review of Previous Class — The Transformation of Religious Worldview

#### Main Idea

The instructor recaps three fundamental shifts in how humans perceive reality, which occurred as Western religious worldview moved from the **mother goddess** → **polytheism** → **monotheism**.

**Shift 1: Mind vs. Matter**

- Previously: mind leads to matter. The universe is one giant mind that vibrates; intersections of vibrations create atoms, which create matter.
- Today: science teaches the reverse — the brain creates the mind. The instructor flags this as "very problematic" and promises to revisit it throughout the semester.

**Shift 2: Metaphorical vs. Literal Understanding**

- Previously: the world was understood metaphorically. Invisible forces were given names — the gods.
- Today: science teaches literalism. If you can't see it or measure it, it doesn't exist.

**Shift 3: Intuitive vs. Counter-Intuitive**

- Previously: humans trusted their heart and their eyes. Knowledge was intuitive.
- Today: science teaches you to distrust your senses and trust data, tools, and theory instead.

> "School is hard because you're taught ideas that go against your own intuition."

**Connection**: These three shifts set up the semester's larger argument — that modern scientific materialism has severed us from older ways of knowing, and this severance has consequences for how power operates.

**Connection**: The instructor emphasizes that Egypt's solution created a unique vulnerability — the pharaoh was human, not actually god, and therefore his life was always at risk. This sets up the entire lecture's argument about why mind control techniques were developed.

### Dissociation — The Master Concept

#### Main Idea

All three leadership skills reduce to one word: **dissociation**. Dissociation means your mind separates from your body — you become an observer watching your own life as if it were a movie. You're in the movie but also watching the movie.

Another term for this: **multiple personality disorder**. Trump is "so unpredictable because he's literally a hundred different people." In different circumstances, he becomes a different person.

#### Supporting Material: Game Theory and Secret Alliances

The instructor connects dissociation to game theory:

- In a game with millions of players, the secret to winning is **cheating through secret alliances**
- Since many people form alliances, the real winner is the person who belongs to **as many powerful secret alliances as possible** — sitting at the intersection of multiple groups
- Secret societies build systems to ensure loyalty and prevent betrayal
- The only way to navigate competing loyalties across multiple groups is to **develop multiple personalities** — you are literally a different person in each context
- Spies are recruited specifically for this skill: the ability to dissociate quickly

**Connection**: This reframes dissociation not as a disorder but as an evolutionary advantage in complex power games. The term used for someone with this capacity is **psychopath**.

### Programming the Pharaoh — Egyptian Mythology as a Script

#### Main Idea

Since the pharaoh was born into his position (not self-selected for psychopathic traits), the priests needed a method to **manufacture dissociation** — to take a person and deliberately create multiple personalities. The instructor argues Egyptian mythology was not a story to be believed but a **script to be acted out**.

> "It's not something to be believed. It's something to be acted out."

#### The Three Main Gods as Three Programmed Identities

| God | Role in Mythology | Programmed Identity |
|---|---|---|
| **Ra** | Sun god who fights the serpent Apophis every night to ensure the sun rises | The **virtuous hero** |
| **Osiris** | God of civilization, tricked and killed by his brother Seth | The **passive victim** |
| **Horus** | Son of Osiris and Isis, fights Seth to reclaim the throne | The **vengeful child** |

#### The Mythology in Detail

**Ra's story**: Every night, Ra descends into the underworld and battles **Apophis**, a serpent trying to swallow the sun. Ra kills Apophis through various methods — stabbing, lancing, burning, strangling. If Ra fails one night, the result is a solar eclipse.

**Osiris's story**: Seth, Osiris's jealous brother, tricks him into lying in a tomb, seals it, and throws it away. **Isis** (Osiris's wife) searches for him. Seth then cuts Osiris into pieces and scatters them around the world. Isis finds all the pieces, reassembles him, and quickly conceives Horus with him before Seth can strike again.

**Horus's story**: Horus challenges Seth to reclaim the throne through a series of contests — including turning into hippos and trying to drown each other, and sexual domination by Seth. Horus eventually wins and becomes pharaoh.

#### How the Script Was Enacted

The instructor speculates (and emphasizes this is speculation) that these myths were **ritually enacted on the pharaoh** while under the influence of psychedelics:

- **As Ra**: The drugged pharaoh, dressed as Ra, would stab, lance, burn, or strangle an effigy or real person — training the "virtuous hero" identity
- **As Osiris**: The pharaoh would be placed in a tomb, cut, and subjected to sexual acts — training the "passive victim" identity
- **As Horus**: The pharaoh would undergo drowning and sexual domination — training the "vengeful child" identity

**The role of Isis as controller**: Throughout the trauma, a priest wearing an Isis mask and using an Isis voice would comfort the pharaoh. This created a dependency — Isis becomes the **controller**, the one person the pharaoh always trusts. Later, the priest in the Isis mask could whisper instructions to the pharaoh while he slept or was drugged.

**Activation mechanism — smell**: Different scents were associated with different identities. When the priests needed the pharaoh to behave a certain way in the throne room, they would release the corresponding scent to activate that identity and its associated emotions.

### The Philosophical Basis — Kant and the Noumena

#### Main Idea

The instructor invokes **Kant** to explain why this programming works. Kant distinguishes between:

- **Noumena** — things in themselves (true reality, unknowable)
- **Phenomena** — things as we perceive them

Since we cannot differentiate between true and false experience, **controlling someone's experience means controlling their memories**. This is what makes ritual programming possible.

#### Supporting Material: Monkey Island Thought Experiment

A previously discussed thought experiment: 100 people are randomly transported to an island where they must survive flesh-eating monkeys. The experience makes them strong, wise, and united. They return and conquer the world.

The problem: how do you pass this down to the next generation? The answer is **ritual** — you place your children on an island, tell them the story, and give them **psychedelics** (drugs that enhance experience so vividly that you believe the stories actually happened to you).

> "It's like being put into a movie."

**Connection**: This is what the instructor calls **programming** — the "great secret of the human brain" that every human brain can be programmed through controlled experience.

### Abu Ghraib, Guantanamo, and the Egyptian Script in Modern Times

#### Main Idea

The instructor draws a direct line from ancient Egyptian programming to the US War on Terror.

#### Supporting Material: Abu Ghraib Prison

After 9/11, the US military arrested suspected terrorists and held them at **Abu Ghraib** prison in Iraq. Photos leaked to CBS News showed prisoners being subjected to disturbing abuse. The military's initial explanation: rogue soldiers who "went crazy."

Then it emerged that **two psychologists** designed the program. They were sued by victims. Court testimony revealed they were paid **$80 million by the CIA**.

#### The Theory of Learned Helplessness

The official justification was **"enhanced interrogation"** based on the theory of **learned helplessness** — the idea that you can break someone's will so completely they become compliant. In the instructor's framework: turning Ra (the virtuous hero/fighter) into Osiris (the passive victim).

The psychologist who developed learned helplessness, **Martin Seligman**, also developed **positive psychology**. The instructor's provocative claim:

> "Positive psychology that you're learning in school is brainwashing."

Recommended reading: **_Dark Side_ by Jane Mayer** (New Yorker reporter), which investigates the torture program in depth.

#### Supporting Material: The Creation of ISIS

The instructor argues the program didn't fail — it worked, but not as publicly claimed. Instead of creating compliant prisoners, it created **ISIS** (Islamic State).

Key observation: ISIS shares its name with the Egyptian goddess **Isis** — the controller figure in the mythology. The instructor asks whether this is coincidence.

Evidence cited:

### MK Ultra — The Domestic Program

#### Main Idea

**MK Ultra** was a CIA program in the 1950s-60s led by chemist **Sidney Gottlieb**, designed to test the effects of drugs and brainwashing on American citizens, including children. Long dismissed as conspiracy theory, it was eventually acknowledged by the US government with the caveat: "we did this before, but not today."

Key details:

- Most sensitive documentation was **destroyed by participants**
- Only partial documents survive and are now public
- Techniques included: brainwashing, drugs (especially LSD), hypnosis
- They operated a brothel where subjects were given LSD
- They worked with foreign intelligence to conduct research abroad (things illegal domestically were done in places like Egypt)
- The instructor speculates this foreign research may have contributed to the creation of Al Qaeda

#### The Instructor's Extension to Modern Life

The instructor argues MK Ultra's results were not failures but were hidden, and its findings have "spread throughout society":

- Social media as a mechanism of social control
- Psychiatric medication as a form of chemical control

> "Good news. You're now the pharaoh. Each and every one of you are now the pharaoh. Congratulations."

- **Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID)**: Clinical term for multiple personality disorder; presented as both a weapon and a source of misery
- **Learned helplessness**: Theory that systematic abuse can break someone's will into total compliance; used to justify "enhanced interrogation"
- **MK Ultra**: 1950s-60s CIA program testing drugs and brainwashing on civilians, led by Sidney Gottlieb
- **NDE (Near Death Experience)**: Experiences reported by people close to death — seeing the spirit world, meeting God, undergoing a life review
- **Noumena vs. Phenomena**: Kantian distinction — things as they truly are vs. things as we perceive them; used to argue that controlled experience = controlled reality
- **Positive psychology**: Field developed by Martin Seligman; the instructor frames it as a form of brainwashing connected to learned helplessness research
- **Programming**: Using trauma, psychedelics, and ritual to embed subconscious behaviors and alternate identities that can be activated by triggers (smell, words, etc.)
