# Secret History #23 — The Organization of Evil

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6XGAc_OqCbU
**Series:** Predictive History — Secret History

## Overview

Argues Paul, not Jesus, founded Christianity. A wealthy Hellenized Jew trained in sophistry, Paul makes the foundational sophist's move — shifting focus from Jesus' words to Jesus' person — and syncretizes three cultures into a scalable institution: Jewish eschatology (Jesus as messiah of peace, not war), the Greek Dionysus mystery cult (virgin mother, sacrificial death, resurrection, the Eucharist as cannibal-ritual body-and-blood), and the Roman paterfamilias (bishop-cardinal-follower hierarchy). The result is a franchise: Jesus' message to slaves becomes Paul's pitch to patriarchs, who gain a more obedient workforce terrified of hell and a spiritual storehouse for their gold.

The lecturer contrasts Jesus' three teachings (self-salvation, independent thinking, rich cannot enter heaven) with Paul's inversions (belief-based redemption, conformity, rich underwrite the kingdom), reads Acts's Road to Damascus as a Plato's Cave imitation and Paul's Jerusalem/Rome episodes as a Roman agent (or double agent) at work, and dissects Paul's letters — circumcision of the heart, faith over law, slavery reframed, "I become all things," 1 Corinthians 13's love-as-obedience. Augustine's City of God systemizes the inversion (pride as the original sin, the divine spark is Satanic), and Ray Kroc's McDonald's franchise is the closing analogy for how Paul sold patriarchs on the model.

## Key themes

- Paul as a Hellenized Jewish rhetorician and likely Roman or double-agent spy
- Philosopher vs. sophist: shift from Jesus' words to Jesus' person as the founding trick
- Three-culture syncretism: Jewish eschatology, Dionysus mystery cult, Roman paterfamilias
- The Eucharist's Dionysian cannibal-ritual roots and its destruction of individuality
- Three sales pitches: patriarchs get obedient workers, gold-storage, and fast expansion
- Jesus vs. Paul on self-salvation, independent thinking, and wealth
- Acts's Road to Damascus read as Plato's Cave imitation; Jerusalem/Rome as agent operations
- Close readings of Paul: circumcision of the heart, faith over law, 1 Corinthians' sophistries
- Augustine's City of God and the inversion of the divine spark into Satanic pride
- Ray Kroc / McDonald's franchise as the structural analogy for Pauline Christianity
