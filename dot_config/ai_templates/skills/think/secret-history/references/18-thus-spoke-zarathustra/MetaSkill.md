# Secret History #18 — Thus Spoke Zarathustra

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dELEdxh0ceg
**Series:** Predictive History — Secret History

## Overview

Argues Zoroastra is the most influential person who ever lived. Writing in late Bronze Age northern Iran — a brutal mining colony — he collapses a rotten polytheism into a structured duality: Ahura Mazda (Lord of Wisdom, represented by fire) vs. the Lie, and Asha (truth/virtue) vs. Druj. The Gathas formalize individual judgment, free will, and good thoughts / good words / good deeds; this becomes the structural template for Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.

The lecturer uses Kant's Categorical Imperative (universality, free will, humans as ends) to illuminate Asha, and traces its echoes through Plato's Allegory of the Cave (return to free the prisoners), Rumi's poetry, and Nietzsche's Thus Spoke Zarathustra. Nietzsche's Zarathustra leaves his mountain cave to share wisdom, insists virtue must be brewed from vice, demands we burn ourselves to ashes to learn, and savages both organized religion and academic priesthood. A sharp contrast with Buddhism closes the case: Asha requires action, not just compassion — indifference is complicity. Ego and fear are the twin obstacles, and becoming is the only endpoint.

## Key themes

- Zoroastra as architect of the first monotheism and template for Judaism, Christianity, Islam
- Ahura Mazda (fire, wisdom) and the Asha/Druj duality as the engine of Zoroastrian ethics
- The Gathas: individual judgment, free will, unity of thought/word/deed
- Kant's Categorical Imperative (universality, free will, humans as ends) as a key to Asha
- Plato's Allegory of the Cave: truth obligates returning to free others
- Rumi as spiritual reincarnation of Zoroastra — one beloved beneath all religions
- Nietzsche's Zarathustra descending from the cave, brewing virtue from vice
- "Burn yourself to ashes" as the path: destroy your teachers to rebuild knowledge
- Attack on organized religion and the academic priesthood as betrayals of Asha
- Zoroastrianism vs. Buddhism: action required; indifference is complicity; ego and fear block Asha
