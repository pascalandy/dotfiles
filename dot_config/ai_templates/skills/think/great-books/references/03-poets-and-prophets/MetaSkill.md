# Great Books #3 — Poets and Prophets

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XRP407WsA0w
**Series:** Predictive History — Great Books

## Overview

Homer is framed as the creator of Greek civilization itself — not merely a poet, but the architect of the values that shaped the Greeks: *aretē* (excellence/virtue) and *eudaimonia* (flourishing). The lecture then pairs this with Shelley's *Defence of Poetry* to argue that poets are prophets and unacknowledged legislators of the world.

## Key themes

- Aretē and eudaimonia as Greek civilization's core concepts
- Speech-making as a form of war-fighting
- Techniques of memorable speech in Homer
- Kant revisited: nature of reality, why poetry matters
- Homer as creator of civilization (not just chronicler)
- Shelley's *A Defence of Poetry* — key arguments
- Poetry as gateway to the divine
- Poets as prophets and unacknowledged legislators
