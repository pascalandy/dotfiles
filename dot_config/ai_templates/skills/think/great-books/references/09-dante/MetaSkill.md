# Great Books #9 — Dante

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4EZUrGPgAos (re-upload with audio fixed)
**Series:** Predictive History — Great Books

## Overview

Introductory lecture on Dante's *Divine Comedy*, positioned as a direct response to Virgil's *Aeneid*: love reinstalled at the centre of the moral universe. The lecture opens the *Inferno*, explores why Dante called his work *La Comedia*, and tracks the first descent through Limbo, Minos, and the Circle of Lust.

## Key themes

- Why Dante called the work *La Comedia*
- The Divine Comedy as a response to the Aeneid
- Structure of the Divine Comedy (Inferno / Purgatorio / Paradiso)
- Paradox and cognitive dissonance as method
- Dante's biographical context
- Opening of the *Inferno* — the shadowed forest
- Beatrice, Lucia, and the problem of reciprocity
- Charon at the river — who has authority in Hell?
- Will and desire: why people are in Hell
- Limbo: Virgil's self-justification
- Minos: "don't trust anyone here"
- The Circle of Lust — Virgil refuses to name Dido
- The nature of the journey
