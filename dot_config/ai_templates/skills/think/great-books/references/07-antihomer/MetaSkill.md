# Great Books #7 — The Anti-Homer

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EBWTRvjZ1dw
**Series:** Predictive History — Great Books

## Overview

Opening lecture on Virgil's *Aeneid*, framed as a deliberate inversion of Homer. Where the *Iliad* elevates love and empathy, the *Aeneid* elevates piety and duty to empire. The lecture argues that Virgil's project is ideological: rewriting the Greek moral universe into a Roman one.

## Key themes

- Homer as the infrastructure of Greek civilization
- Romans vs. Greeks — a clash of values
- The Aeneid's grand structure and imperial purpose
- The fall of Troy: Greek *culture* itself as the Trojan Horse
- The death of Priam — negating the Iliad's moral lesson
- Helen and the inversion of love
- The death of Creusa: wifely duty as self-sacrifice
- Heaven as piety, not love
- The Aeneid's long shadow on Western civilization
