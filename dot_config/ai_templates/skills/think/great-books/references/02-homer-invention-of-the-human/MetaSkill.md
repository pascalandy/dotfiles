# Great Books #2 — Homer and the Invention of the Human

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ft2CuowGuYc
**Series:** Predictive History — Great Books

## Overview

How Homer's *Iliad* "invents" the human being by depicting, for the first time in literature, a character (Achilles) arguing with himself — the birth of self-reflective consciousness on the page. Borrowing from Harold Bloom, the lecture argues that great books *make us human* by acting as mirrors that let us see ourselves.

## Key themes

- The Achilles exercise: great books as mirrors
- Harold Bloom's claim that great books help us become human
- The Agamemnon–Achilles argument as consciousness in action
- The mystery of how Homer *did this* — creation vs. craft
- The "standard model" of identity: memory + emotion
- Speculative theory: universal consciousness and archetypes
- Prophet, poet, teacher as the same function
- Why the *Iliad* is the foundation of Western self-awareness
