# Great Books #8 — The Poetry of Empire

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YXTRlvFIRt8
**Series:** Predictive History — Great Books

## Overview

Concluding lecture on the *Aeneid*, centered on Dido and Turnus as sacrifices to piety. The lecture argues that Aeneas's arc is the opposite of Odysseus's: where Odysseus reunites in love, Aeneas disintegrates love in the name of empire. Ends with a preview of Dante as the answer to Virgil.

## Key themes

- The Aeneid as anti-Homer (recap and extension)
- Preview: how to read the *Divine Comedy*
- Mercury's warning to Aeneas
- The Odyssey inverted: love as destination vs. love as obstacle
- Dido confronts Aeneas — love as disintegration
- Aeneas's response: denial and power
- Dido's curse and suicide; Aeneas sleeps in peace
- The death of Turnus — inverting the Iliad's ending
- The ending as epiphany: Aeneas becomes fully pious
- Iliad vs. Aeneid on vengeance and love
- Apollo and Bacchus: two modes of creativity
