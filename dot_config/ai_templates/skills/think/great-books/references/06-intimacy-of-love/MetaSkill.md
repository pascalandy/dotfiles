# Great Books #6 — The Intimacy of Love

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aS-NfPSPMu8
**Series:** Predictive History — Great Books

## Overview

Concluding lecture on the *Odyssey*, centered on love as the unifying force that restores identity. Penelope and Odysseus's reunion is read as a three-stage reconstruction of the self through intimacy — culminating in the bed scene and the teaching from Achilles in the underworld about the purpose of life.

## Key themes

- The Odyssey as a story of homecoming and depression
- Love as a force of unity
- The three planes of consciousness
- Love as intimacy and "secret language"
- Contrast: Helen and Menelaus as what love is *not*
- The brooch scene — Odysseus and Penelope's coded conversation
- The bow competition as resurrection of identity
- The stringing of the bow
- The bed scene: final reconciliation
- Achilles in the underworld on the purpose of life
