# Great Books #4 — The Conscious Universe

**Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dkzr5A8IlLA
**Series:** Predictive History — Great Books

## Overview

Concluding lecture on the *Iliad*, arguing that the epic's final movements dramatize the soul's capacity to expand through empathy and perspective. The centerpiece is the meeting of Priam and Achilles — where the enemy's father kneels before the killer, and the universe "intervenes" through shared humanity.

- Achilles' hidden sabotage of Patroclus
- Models of consciousness functioning at multiple levels
- The Greek metaphor: layers of consciousness
- The Shield of Achilles as the soul-as-universe
- Achilles after killing Hector: guilt and madness
- Priam before Achilles — the greatest battle in the Iliad
- Andromache's lament and the power of perspective
- Reincarnation, empathy, and the purpose of great books
